# She built a Claude shopping assistant to stop buying cheap junk

> **Note on speaker labels:** The interview-transcriber's auto-attribution swapped speakers in many middle segments. Labels below have been corrected manually using content cues (Nicole = built the system, lives in Brooklyn, has second baby; Claire = host, founder of ChatPRD, lives in San Francisco).

## Summary

Nicole Ruiz, a writer and parent who covers technology in the household on her Substack *The Third Oikos*, joins Claire Vo to demonstrate a Claude Project she built for buying high-quality, long-lasting goods. The system encodes her family's purchasing values — natural materials, heritage manufacturers, repairability, return policies — and curates results from trusted vendors like Boston General Store, L.L.Bean, and Manufactum rather than the noise of Amazon, paid ads, and drop-shipping operations.

The episode walks through the Claude Project's custom instructions, a live demo shopping for a can opener (which surfaces a Kuhn Rikon from a near-century-old manufacturer), and how Claude Cowork connects to Gmail to find receipts and draft refund-request emails when products fail. Claire frames the technique as moving an "invisible checklist" of purchasing criteria into a "visible checklist" inside a Claude Project — what she calls an "anti-to-do list" that takes the mental overhead out of household procurement.

The broader thesis: AI is a "force leveler" for small artisans and century-old heritage brands whose websites are terrible but whose products outlast modern direct-to-consumer brands. By offloading the administrative toil of vetting, purchasing, and returning, parents reclaim time for the human parts of parenting instead of being the "human link" between hard-to-navigate online systems.
