# Satya Nadella, Dwarkesh Patel, Dylan Patel – scaling, scaffolding, and the sovereignty scam

## Summary

This Turing Post "Attention Span" episode provides an analytical breakdown of Dwarkesh Patel's interview with Satya Nadella, CEO of Microsoft, with Dylan Patel from SemiAnalysis as co-interviewer. The host highlights that this is a rare instance where a corporate CEO is genuinely challenged by pointed questions, noting that Nadella's body language often reveals more than his carefully crafted corporate responses. The interview took place at Microsoft's Fairwater 2 facility in Atlanta, described as possibly the most powerful AI facility in the world.

The analysis distills Microsoft's AI strategy as articulated by Nadella: the "wrapper era" is dead, replaced by deep native integrations; Microsoft will use OpenAI models for seven more years while developing their own MAI family; Azure is becoming model-agnostic; and Microsoft's long-term vision is to become infrastructure for autonomous agents. The host identifies several contradictions in Nadella's statements, particularly around multi-model futures versus exclusive OpenAI leverage, and claims about globalization ending while simultaneously pitching US-led AI factories worldwide.

The most intense moments come when Dylan Patel presses Nadella on Microsoft's capacity decisions, questioning why they paused data center expansion while competitors like Oracle, Google, Meta, and AWS snapped up those sites. The host suggests that Nadella's body language during these exchanges reveals more about Microsoft's strategic concerns than his verbal answers do.
