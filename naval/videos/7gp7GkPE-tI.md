# Pause, Reflect, See How Well it Did

## Summary

In this short but dense clip, Naval Ravikant lays out the fundamental process of learning and mastery. The core idea is that you should keep redefining what you do until you are the best in the world at it, and the only way to get there is through iteration -- not mechanical repetition, but genuine learning loops. He draws a sharp distinction between "10,000 hours" (raw time) and "10,000 iterations" (cycles of doing, pausing, reflecting, and changing).

Naval connects this to universal systems of progress: biological evolution (mutation, replication, selection), technology (innovation, scaling, market survival), and the scientific method as described by David Deutsch (conjecture, criticism, weeding out). The endpoint is finding what is natural for yourself through a high-agency process of living in the arena, iterating until your work aligns so perfectly with who you are that being the best is simply being yourself.
