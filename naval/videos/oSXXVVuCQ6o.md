# Blame Yourself for Everything, and Preserve Your Agency

## Summary

Naval Ravikant and Nivi unpack Naval's tweet: "Blame yourself for everything, and preserve your agency." The core argument is that taking full responsibility for your circumstances is the only way to maintain the power to change them. If a problem is someone else's fault, you have surrendered your ability to fix it. Nivi frames the discussion by noting that people retweet things when they encounter an idea they already knew implicitly but had never seen articulated -- what Emerson called recognizing "our own rejected thoughts" returning "with a certain alienated majesty."

Naval then tackles the cynicism that leads people to blame external forces -- bankers, oligarchs, race, geography, disability -- for their station in life. While acknowledging the world is not a level playing field, he argues that luck's influence recedes dramatically over long time frames when paired with intense effort and iteration. He points to Silicon Valley as proof: every brilliant person he met there 20 years ago is now successful, and none of them were born there -- they moved there because they wanted to be high-agency. The conversation concludes with a powerful metaphor: pessimism is like riding a motorcycle while staring at the brick wall you are supposed to avoid. You will inevitably crash into whatever you focus on. Children are born with agency; the challenge is preserving it.
