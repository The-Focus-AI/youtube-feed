# Macbook Neo Review: Better than you Think!

## Summary

After a full week with the MacBook Neo, MKBHD finds Apple's $599 entry-level laptop to be significantly more capable than he initially expected. Powered by a binned A18 Pro chip from the iPhone 16 Pro, the Neo benchmarks at M1 levels of performance across Geekbench, Cinebench, and GPU tests, with single-core performance actually closer to M3. Web browsing, email, word processing, spreadsheets, and even light photo editing all run smoothly. The 8GB of RAM fills up quickly, but Apple Silicon's swap memory management keeps the experience smooth for typical use, just as it did on the original M1 MacBook Air.

Marques systematically rates the Neo across different user categories: A+ for K-12 students, A for college students and writers, B+ for podcasters, B for coders, B- for photographers (limited by the display not covering full P3 color), and a definitive "not for gaming" for gamers. He also tests video editing in Final Cut Pro, finding basic 4K edits surprisingly manageable but plugins and heavier workflows quickly hitting the wall of the machine's limitations.

Perhaps most striking is Marques's analysis of why this laptop is so easy for Apple to make. They already mass-produce A-series chips by the millions for iPhones, driving unit costs ridiculously low. He points out that Apple threw an A19 Pro chip with 12GB of RAM into the new Studio Display just to run its webcam and speakers -- more RAM than the Neo itself. He predicts the next version of the Neo will get an A19 Pro with 12GB RAM, True Tone via an ambient light sensor, and a keyboard backlight. His conclusion: this is 90-something percent of a MacBook Air for hundreds of dollars less.
