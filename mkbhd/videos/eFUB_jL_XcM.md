# So This is Peak Foldable

## Summary

Marques Brownlee declares "peak foldable" with the Oppo Find N6, the seven-years-into-the-Galaxy-Fold-1-era device that he says has finally engineered away every meaningful compromise of the form factor. At 230 grams and just under 9mm thick, the N6 is dimensionally identical to the iPhone 17 Pro Max -- meaning the cover screen is now a perfectly normal slab phone you actually want to use one-handed instead of immediately opening up.

He walks through how each prior compromise has been solved: a 6,000mAh silicon-carbon battery with 80W wired and 50W wireless charging fits despite the hinge; the 200MP ISOCELL HP5 sensor (also in the S25 Edge) plus 50MP ultrawide and 50MP 3x telephoto deliver flagship cameras in a thin Z-axis; a slightly-binned Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 plus a dedicated S1 networking chip handles performance; IP59 rating handles durability. And the centerpiece -- the "no-feel crease" -- uses laser-scanned titanium hinges with 3D-printed liquid polymer fill (UV-cured) to drop surface variation from 0.2mm to 0.05mm, "less than half a human hair thickness," rated for 600,000 folds.

There's also pen support via a magnetic case that wirelessly charges the stylus from the phone's battery. Brownlee closes by pivoting to the inevitable: now that foldables are mature, Apple is the obvious next entrant -- and rumors point to a "passport style" iPhone fold with a short squat 5.5" cover screen that opens into a near-8" widescreen "portable iPad Mini," due possibly later this year.
