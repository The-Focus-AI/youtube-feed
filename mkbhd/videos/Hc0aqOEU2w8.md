# Bluey Phone Review

## Summary

In this April Fools' Day parody, Marques Brownlee gives a deadpan flagship-style review treatment to the $10.99 VTech Bluey Ring Phone -- a children's toy phone modeled after characters from the Australian kids' show. Brownlee evaluates the unboxing, hardware, and feature set with all the rigor he'd apply to a real flagship, noting the open-faced cardboard packaging, the absence of a charger or replacement battery tool, and the surprisingly large 1.7-inch black-and-white display.

The bit lands because Brownlee plays it completely straight: he frames the device as the ultimate "minimal phone" precisely because it has no internet, no Bluetooth, no cell radios, and no cameras whatsoever. He praises the speaker grille's thoughtful placement, the under-1-second boot time, and the bubble-blowing microphone game as a "much healthier" alternative to doomscrolling TikTok or Instagram. The chat feature, he notes approvingly, is not AI -- the only conversation partners available are Bluey and Bingo the dogs.

His verdict: while expensive minimalist phones still cave and include touchscreens, cameras, and apps ("cowards"), the Bluey phone genuinely commits. With sub-1,000 total pixels, sub-4 frames per second, and zero connectivity, it reduces total doomscrolling to effectively zero. He signs off with a directive to "check the date."
