# iOS 27 Hands-On: Top 5 New Features!

## Summary

Marques Brownlee walks through the top five features of iOS 27 after running the developer and public betas since WWDC. He frames the release as "the update of refinement" — no big sweeping changes, but a pile of little things people have been waiting on for a long time. He also notes it has been among the most stable set of Apple developer betas he's tested in years.

The countdown runs from generative-AI photo editing (Extend and Spatial Reframe) at number five, through a "freaking finally" bucket of long-overdue fixes (separate alarm volume, AirPods custom EQ, CarPlay scrubbing, better Wi-Fi-to-cellular handoff) at four, aesthetic tweaks including a liquid-glass opacity slider at three, and a ground-up Siri overhaul — built "with the help of Google" — at number two. The new Siri is conversational, multimodal, screen-aware, and indexes personal data across Apple apps to answer questions like "What was that watch Dave sent me?"

The number one feature, in his ranking, is raw speed: system-wide performance optimizations and a new CPU scheduler that make everything feel snappier — most dramatically on older hardware, with compatibility stretching all the way back to the 2019 iPhone 11. He closes by strongly recommending everyone install the update when it lands.
