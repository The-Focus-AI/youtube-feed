# Nothing Phone 4A/Pro Review: I Have a Theory

## Summary

MKBHD reviews the Nothing Phone 4A and 4A Pro, two mid-range phones that represent a strategic pivot for Nothing as a company. The 4A starts at 349 Euros for European markets while the 4A Pro comes in at $499 for the US. Notably absent is a "Nothing Phone 4" flagship, which Brownlee attributes to Nothing's inability to compete on flagship specs against Samsung, Apple, and Xiaomi given their smaller order volumes and rising RAM prices.

The 4A features Nothing's signature semi-transparent design in a striking matte blue with triple cameras, a Snapdragon 7s Gen 2, and Nothing OS 4.1 on Android 16. The 4A Pro steps up with a unibody aluminum body, a Snapdragon 7 Gen 4, and an upgraded pixel-dot glyph matrix on the back for customizable notifications. Both phones run refined software that Brownlee praises for its snappiness and unique home screen customization through the new community-driven Playground widget store.

However, Brownlee notes that many of the 4A Pro's "flagship-style" specs -- 144Hz refresh rate, 5,000-nit brightness, 140x zoom -- are largely marketing numbers that offer negligible real-world improvement over the standard 4A. He concludes that the 4A Pro is less of a true "Pro" and more of a slightly up-badged version for the US market, while the standard 4A represents the better value proposition overall.
