# Samsung Galaxy S26 Ultra Review: There's a Catch

## Summary

MKBHD delivers his full review of the Samsung Galaxy S26 Ultra, framing the entire phone around the concept of trade-offs. The headline feature is the Privacy Display, a genuinely innovative technology that uses two types of pixels -- wide-angle and narrow-angle -- to instantly limit viewing angles on demand, preventing anyone beside you from seeing your screen. However, this comes at significant cost: when privacy mode is on, half the pixels turn off, cutting resolution in half with visibly blockier edges. Even with privacy off, viewing angles are slightly worse all the time because of the narrow focusing lenses on half the pixels. Samsung also stuck with an 8-bit display panel rather than the 10-bit panels appearing in competitors.

The design gets thinner, lighter, and rounder, but this too introduces trade-offs. The rounded corners mean the S Pen slot is now capped with a slight curve instead of being flat, and the camera plateau causes the phone to rock viciously on a table. Performance is solid with the Snapdragon chip showing slightly higher benchmarks than its predecessor, and battery life is marginally improved with a 5,000 mAh battery, but Marques is frustrated that Samsung still has no Qi2 magnets and still has not significantly improved charging speed. He cites Mrwhosetheboss's battery test showing the S26 Ultra improving over last gen but falling behind dedicated battery phones.

On cameras, the bigger apertures on the main and 5X lenses let in more light with slightly softer backgrounds, and a new super aggressive video stabilization mode produces impressively smooth Quad HD footage. The AI software features are described as ranging from genuinely useful (call screening, Audio Eraser) to "slop" (Photo Assist letting users fabricate scenes). Marques closes by calling out the S26 and S26 Plus as poor values at $900, arguing they have the same cameras since S23, lost millimeter wave, and use Exynos in many markets. The S26 Ultra itself is a good phone whose price is "probably the most ultra thing about it."
