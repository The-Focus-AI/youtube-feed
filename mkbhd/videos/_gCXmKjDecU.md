# WWDC 2026 Impressions: Yeah, That's About Right

## Summary

Filming from a California hotel room right after Apple's WWDC 2026 keynote, Marques Brownlee walks through an event that breezed past the usual OS grab bag to spend its time on two big themes: trust and safety for kids, and the long-awaited new Siri. The quick-hits portion actually earns his praise — instead of another dramatic visual overhaul, iOS 27, iPadOS 27, watchOS, tvOS, and macOS "Golden Gate" mostly look the same while fixing the under-the-hood sloppiness that crept in with liquid glass: smoother animations, faster app launches, better Spotlight indexing, 80% faster AirDrop, matching corner radiuses, a liquid-glass transparency slider nobody asked for, panorama environments on Vision Pro, and — finally — custom EQ for AirPods.

The trust-and-safety section gets a double reading. On its face it's genuinely useful: dedicated child accounts, parental control over apps, websites, and contacts, and granular screen-time scheduling. The cynical read is that "get your mom an iPhone" has become "get your kid an iPhone" — the best possible parental controls mean the youngest possible iPhone users, which means more iPhones in the future. Then comes the headliner: the new Siri is, in Marques's words, exactly what we were expecting — straight down Broadway, no crazy surprises and no letdowns. It launches into a conversational chat with world knowledge and cited sources, syncs history in a new Siri app, and can search and act across Messages, Photos, and Calendar. It deliberately stops short of Google's agentic ticket-buying demos, but its trump card is on-device context that ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude can't touch. Third-party app support hinges on developers adopting App Intents, and the most disappointing slide reveals the newest on-device models only run on the iPhone Air and 17 Pro — the 12GB RAM phones — leaving the "built for Apple Intelligence" iPhone 16 already outdated.

Beyond Siri there's AI tab-grouping and natural-language Safari extensions, describe-it-and-it-builds-it Siri Shortcuts, consolidated Apple Home notifications, photo edge-extension up to 25%, and a spatial reframing trick that bends the definition of a photo. The most Apple thing ever: a Passwords app that agentically logs into sites and changes your weak passwords for you — locking you deeper into the ecosystem in the process. Marques lands on the irony of the macOS name: when the walls of the walled garden are golden gates, maybe it's not so bad in there — unless you're a HomePod user, since that product went entirely unmentioned. And a sneaky final slide about apps dynamically resizing to different aspect ratios reads as "tell me you have a foldable coming without telling me you have a foldable coming."

## Key Points

- **Keynote structure** ([0:26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=26s)) - Apple breezed through OS updates quickly, then focused on two main topics: trust and safety for kids, and Apple Intelligence with the new Siri
- **A cleanup year instead of a flashy one** ([0:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=41s)) - Rather than new feature after new feature, the update combs through neglected details after liquid glass releases that "felt a little sloppy sometimes"
- **macOS is now Golden Gate** ([1:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=77s)) - iOS 27, iPadOS 27, watchOS, tvOS, and macOS mostly look the same but get under-the-hood improvements
- **Quality-of-life fixes** ([1:34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=94s)) - Smoother animations, faster app opening, tweaked app icons, matching corner radiuses, sidebar icon colors back, and a liquid glass transparency slider
- **80% faster AirDrop and better Spotlight** ([1:54](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=114s)) - Sounds amazing, "as long as you can also make AirDrop work more of the time"
- **Vision Pro panorama environments** ([2:18](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=138s)) - Your own panorama shots can now be immersive environments, not just Apple's Mars/Yosemite/Mount Hood set
- **Custom EQ for AirPods, finally** ([2:33](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=153s)) - The world's most popular earbuds catch up to a feature everyone else has had for years
- **Child accounts and granular screen time** ([3:25](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=205s)) - Parents control app downloads, websites, and contacts, with schedulers for when app categories are allowed
- **The iPhone pipeline strategy** ([4:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=261s)) - The best parental controls mean the youngest possible iPhone users, which means more iPhone users in adult life
- **How the new Siri works** ([5:11](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=311s)) - Double tap at the bottom or hold the power button, a new expressive voice, conversational chat with cited world knowledge, and history synced in a new Siri app across the ecosystem
- **Apple's classic ecosystem play** ([6:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=360s)) - ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude are good at other things, but Siri is the one that indexes everything actually on your iPhone
- **Live demos as a trust signal** ([6:51](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=411s)) - A keynote full of live demos read as "we learned our lesson from last time" after the previous Siri debacle
- **Deliberately not agentic** ([7:23](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=443s)) - Unlike Google's Gemini buying concert tickets from a poster photo at I/O, Siri stops short and just adds the event to your calendar
- **Third-party apps need App Intents** ([9:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=540s)) - Siri defaults to Apple apps but can be summoned into Spotify, Pocket Casts, or others if the developer enabled App Intents and you name the app
- **Newest on-device models are iPhone Air and 17 Pro only** ([10:13](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=613s)) - The 12GB RAM requirement leaves the iPhone 16 behind; other iPhones miss only the new voice and updated dictation
- **Siri as a floating orb in Vision Pro** ([10:45](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=645s)) - Eye tracking means you just look at the orb and talk, and it can answer about windows or real objects around you
- **Natural-language Safari extensions and Siri Shortcuts** ([11:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=675s)) - Describe an extension or shortcut and it builds it, editable afterward — same as Google's home automations move
- **Photo extension and spatial reframing** ([12:13](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=733s)) - Extend image edges up to 25% and rotate perspective with AI-inferred depth, the closest Apple has come to bending what a photo is
- **Cloud features are metered through iCloud** ([13:12](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=792s)) - The powerful cloud model features have daily limits unless you upgrade your iCloud subscription
- **The agentic Passwords app** ([13:22](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=802s)) - It finds weak or compromised passwords, logs into the site, changes them for you, and locks you further into the ecosystem
- **No HomePod mention at all** ([14:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gCXmKjDecU&t=870s)) - The little Siri box with nice speakers went entirely unacknowledged the whole event
