# Nothing Phone 4b: They Can't Say It!

## Summary

Marques Brownlee reviews Nothing's newest budget phone and spends the whole video poking at the one thing Nothing refuses to say out loud: this is the cheap one. He opens with a callback to the Nothing Phone (2a) Plus naming saga — a canned statement from Nothing's Head of Marketing that used "a lot of marketing words" to explain absolutely nothing about what the letter suffixes mean — and diagnoses the company with an "allergy to just calling this a cheaper budget phone." The phone itself costs 330 euros / 300 pounds, skips the US entirely, and lands in genuinely competitive budget territory in India and Europe. Marques also floats a new theory: this phone was originally supposed to be a CMF Phone Pro from Nothing's sub-$300 sub-brand, but the memory price spike pushed the bill of materials high enough that Nothing converted it into a slightly more premium Nothing-branded phone instead.

The hardware is a study in what Marques calls a "balancing act of including premium fundamentals, but then like finding ways to save money in ways that you either won't notice or won't mind." The 6.7-inch 120Hz LTPO AMOLED is genuinely good, but the bezels are uneven, the anti-glare coating is worse than premium phones, and the optical in-display fingerprint reader sits low and reads slow. The two cameras — a 50MP OIS primary on a physically tiny Samsung sensor plus an 8MP ultra-wide — are "good enough to not be a dealbreaker at this price" and nothing more. The Snapdragon 6 Gen 4 benchmarks near the seven-year-old OnePlus 7 Pro, yet Nothing OS 4.1's optimization and a 1000Hz touch sample rate make the software feel like it overperforms the chip, putting the phone at the top of its price class for responsiveness.

The verdict framework is simple: a list of perks (high refresh OLED, IP64, true stereo speakers, glyph lights, and a 5200mAh battery — 6000mAh in India — that's the biggest ever in a Nothing phone and easily lasts a day and a half) against a list of shortcomings (no wireless charging, no NFC in the Indian version, a mushy vibration motor, scuff-prone plastic, and slower UFS 2.2 storage that won't age well). If the perks interest you and none of the shortcomings are dealbreakers, put it at the top of your list. Marques closes with a plea: Nothing should just embrace having a lineup that's "cheap, cheaper, cheapest" — just because the B stands for budget doesn't make it a bad thing.

## Key Points

- **The naming non-answer** ([0:18](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=18s)) - Nothing's Head of Marketing explains the lettering system without ever saying what the letter actually stands for: "Numbers represent generations, while letters indicate different product segments"
- **Marques's decode** ([0:42](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=42s)) - His sarcastic best guess at the lineup: flagship, cheaper phone, and a slightly more premium version of that
- **Price and availability** ([1:03](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=63s)) - Exactly 330 euros or 300 pounds; not launching in the US, but competitive in India and Europe
- **The no-flagship theory holds** ([1:22](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=82s)) - Nothing's costs are too high at their volume to make a competitive high-end flagship, and they still don't
- **New theory: converted CMF phone** ([1:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=106s)) - The memory price spike pushed the bill of materials too high for a sub-$300 CMF phone, so it became a pricier Nothing phone
- **Design** ([1:55](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=115s)) - 6.7 inches, transparent top-half window with exposed screws and metal-looking pieces, glyph lights, all-plastic body that at least looks glassy up top
- **Display specs** ([2:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=155s)) - 6.7-inch AMOLED, 120Hz, LTPO, above 1080p but short of 1440p; grey, black, and white colorways with black buttons
- **The cost-saving compromises on screen** ([3:20](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=200s)) - High refresh rate and high-frequency PWM dimming, but uneven bezels, worse anti-glare finish, and a low, slow optical fingerprint reader
- **Two cameras, the right two** ([4:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=240s)) - One primary plus one ultra-wide is "a solid one-two punch. I might argue the correct choice for two cameras"
- **Camera reality check** ([4:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=261s)) - 50MP primary with OIS on a tiny Samsung sensor: acceptable in excellent light, dull colors and noise when the light goes away; the 8MP ultra-wide is never sharp in the corners
- **Chip benchmarks like 2019** ([5:01](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=301s)) - The Snapdragon 6 Gen 4 lands around the seven-year-old OnePlus 7 Pro and close to the Tensor G2, though with a better GPU and a modern vapor chamber
- **Software overperforms the silicon** ([5:44](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=344s)) - Nothing OS 4.1 with a new custom CPU scheduler makes the phone feel smoother than the chip should allow
- **Top of its price class for feel** ([6:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=360s)) - 1000Hz touch sample rate plus optimized software equals class-leading responsiveness, with only occasional stutters
- **No software features missing** ([6:20](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=380s)) - All the Nothing widgets, dots, customization, icon packs, AI toolkit, and Essential Space come to the cheap phone too
- **The decision framework** ([7:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=420s)) - Perks you want plus no dealbreaker shortcomings means put it at the top of your list
- **The perks list** ([7:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=441s)) - High refresh OLED at this price, IP64 rating, true stereo speakers with a dedicated top driver
- **The battery highlight** ([7:47](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=467s)) - 5200mAh (6000mAh in India), a day-and-a-half phone, 33W charging; biggest battery ever in a Nothing phone
- **Glyph lights persist** ([8:08](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=488s)) - Brighter than the 4a's, still notification LEDs and a blinking red recording light
- **The shortcomings list** ([8:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=510s)) - No wireless charging, no NFC in the Indian version, a very mushy vibration motor, and plastic that scuffs early
- **Longevity concerns** ([9:14](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DUgPFNRmsCQ&t=554s)) - UFS 2.2 storage, optical fingerprint reader, and the Snapdragon 6 Gen 4 probably won't have the longest shelf life
