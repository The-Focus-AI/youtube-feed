# Apple Studio Display XDR Review: We're All Pros Now!

## Summary

MKBHD reviews Apple's new Studio Display XDR, a 27-inch 5K mini-LED monitor that effectively replaces the discontinued Pro Display XDR. The new display features 2,000 nits of peak HDR brightness, 2,000 local dimming zones, a 120Hz adaptive sync refresh rate, and Thunderbolt 5 connectivity -- all for a starting price of $3,299 including the stand. Compared to the $5,000 Pro Display XDR (which required a separate $1,000 stand), the Studio Display XDR is brighter, has better contrast, and adds built-in speakers and a 1080p webcam that its predecessor lacked.

Beyond the hardware specs, Marques explores what the existence of this display reveals about Apple's broader strategy. The fact that "Studio" is replacing "Pro" at the top of Apple's lineup signals a deliberate shift away from niche professional products toward high-end consumer devices with pro-level capabilities. He draws a direct connection to the Mac Pro, which has not been updated since M2 Ultra in 2023, predicting its eventual demise in favor of the Mac Studio.

For the MacBook Pro owner looking for the ideal desk companion, Marques argues this is the definitive choice -- a one-cable setup that charges at 140 watts, supports daisy-chaining multiple displays, and finally matches the MacBook Pro's own high-refresh-rate mini-LED panel quality. The nanotexture glass option has also improved significantly since its debut on the original Pro Display XDR, and it is now $300 rather than $1,000 extra.
