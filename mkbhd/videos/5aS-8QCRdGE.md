# The Unreleased Rollable Smartphone!

## Summary

Marques Brownlee gets his hands on the legendary unreleased LG Rollable -- the phone LG was reportedly weeks away from launching when they shut down their entire mobile division in 2021. The unit, sourced (somehow) by dbrand, has functional hardware, working firmware, and a polished software experience. Brownlee frames it as the perfect epitaph for LG: an absurd engineering moonshot from a company that kept making "crazy" experimental phones (the LG Wing, the dual-screen G8X) without ever building the steady money-maker needed to keep the business afloat.

The concept is wild: a regular-looking 6.7" slab phone that, with a three-finger swipe, motors itself open into a 7.4" mini-tablet by rolling its flexible OLED out from behind a side rail. The display wraps around the back through a clear glass panel so you can see notifications and use the rear cameras for selfies. There are no traditional volume buttons (pressure-sensitive zones replace them); the power button doubles as the rear fingerprint reader; the motors are strong enough to push a 16-inch MacBook Pro across a table; and an LG engineer even designed a custom unroll sound to mask the motor noise.

Brownlee notes the obvious caveats: water and dust resistance look impossible (you can see exposed gear teeth and air gaps), the flexible screen is constantly exposed to the elements, and there's still a faint crease where the panel curves around the side. There's a 4,500mAh battery, 12GB of RAM, two motors, and three spring-loaded arms inside. He gives a shout-out to LG and points at Samsung as the only other company still rumored to be exploring the form factor.
