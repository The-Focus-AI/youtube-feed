# Macbook Neo Impressions: Reincarnated!

## Summary

MKBHD gives his first impressions of the MacBook Neo, Apple's newest and cheapest entry to the MacBook lineup at $599. Powered by a binned A18 Pro chip (the same one from the iPhone 16 Pro but with a five-core GPU instead of six), 8 gigs of RAM, and 256 gigs of starting storage, it is a 13-inch all-aluminum laptop available in four colors: indigo, blush, citrus, and silver. It has two USB-C ports (not Thunderbolt), a headphone jack, a 1080p webcam with no notch, and side-firing stereo speakers. Marques describes it as "a Chromebook, but the Apple one" -- a super basic computer for web browsing, email, and everyday tasks.

The video highlights several cost-saving compromises: the base model lacks Touch ID (just a lock button), the trackpad is a mechanical click rather than haptic, the display maxes out at 500 nits and 60Hz, and the laptop weighs the same 2.7 pounds as the slightly larger MacBook Air. Despite these cuts, Marques notes it does not feel cheap, with premium build quality that would likely be the best-built laptop at this price point.

Most intriguingly, MKBHD draws a direct line from the MacBook Neo to the original 12-inch MacBook from about ten years ago -- a machine Apple tried to make but failed because Intel chips were not efficient enough. He argues the MacBook Neo is that same vision "reincarnated," finally made possible by Apple silicon. He also teases that the same logic applies to the Mac Pro being replaced by the Mac Studio, and runs through Apple's other announcements of the week including the Studio Display XDR, iPhone 17e, M5 Pro/Max MacBook Pro updates, and M4 iPad Air.
