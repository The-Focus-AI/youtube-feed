# Traci Donnelly: Using AI to Make an Impact

## Summary

Traci Donnelly — CEO of The Child Center of New York and founder and president of Make An Impact — joins Paul Ford and Rich Ziade to explain how a large human-services nonprofit actually puts AI to work. When she arrived at Child Center about 14 years ago it was a $30 million organization serving 14,000 people; today it runs roughly $120 million across 80 programs in 100 locations, serving about 62,000 people a year, from behavioral health and Head Start to child-abuse prevention and case management. Her thesis, repeated so often that Rich calls her on it, is blunt: a nonprofit is a business. "It requires the head and the heart because we're in the business of people. But you can't serve people if you're not in business. So business comes first."

Donnelly is an unusual client — she pitched AI to Aboard rather than the other way around. She walks through the turnaround that earned her board's trust (a near-miss on payroll, $7,000 in net assets against $6 million owed to the government, then a million-dollar swing in nine months after she invested in an HRIS, a team, and dashboards) and how that same instinct now drives her AI adoption. The wins are concrete and unglamorous: AI scribes that write clinical notes so social workers can be fully present in sessions instead of doing "pajama time" paperwork, and dashboards wrapped in an AI layer so non-technical managers can query their data in plain language without the embarrassment of not knowing how to read a chart. Adoption isn't optional — she treats the tools like buying computers for the org — but she pairs the mandate with relentless dialogue.

The through-line is that AI integration is a leadership and culture problem, not a technical one. Donnelly's advice to hesitant executives: lead with a business case, not an AI case; convert "the language of data into the language of your audience" (a social worker cares about seeing more kids, not about productivity); go to the "why"; and own your mistakes in the same room where you made them. Make An Impact now spans 711 purpose-driven organizations across 25 states, and her ambition is to build a benchmarking data platform the sector has never had — fast, because "if it's long, it's wrong," and because she'd rather show regulators what AI can do than be told what it can't.

## Key Points

- **The dashboard problem AI can fix** ([0:49](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=49s)) - AI spins up dashboards all day, but no one knows how to consume them; the fix is dashboards you can ask questions of and that surface highlights.
- **A client who pitched AI to Aboard** ([1:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=91s)) - Donnelly came in wanting AI-powered dashboards for HIPAA-compliant medical data across orgs — an unusually specific, real ask.
- **The scale of Child Center** ([3:02](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=182s)) - From ~$30M and 14,000 people to ~$120M, 80 programs, 100 locations, and 62,000 people a year over 14 years.
- **Listen to the community, build from the ground up** ([3:26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=206s)) - Serving "zero to seniors," tailoring programs rather than assuming the org knows the answers.
- **An AI fan since the beginning** ([5:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=335s)) - She brought early LLM articles to her board chair, insisting they get ahead of the curve.
- **Nonprofits run on stale data** ([7:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=430s)) - Quarterly data is "history"; real-time KPIs and dashboards are how she turned the org around.
- **Head and heart** ([9:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=540s)) - You need both data and feelings; you can't serve people if you're not in business.
- **Divest what doesn't pay for itself** ([10:08](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=608s)) - She brought the board a P&L and told government funders that underfunded programs must break even before fundraising.
- **The emergency-room turnaround** ([13:59](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=839s)) - $7K net assets, $6M owed, nearly missed payroll; she asked the board to invest $650K in an HRIS, a team, and dashboards.
- **A million-dollar swing** ([15:04](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=904s)) - Clinics projected to lose $750K instead turned a $350K surplus in nine months, building board trust.
- **Stalled AI initiatives everywhere** ([16:03](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=963s)) - Rich diagnoses the hype cycle: "What's our AI strategy?" is the wrong question; "how do I run my business better?" is the right one.
- **AI scribes end the paperwork drag** ([18:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=1080s)) - Note-writing is clinicians' biggest pain point; taking notes during a visit is a "bad vibe."
- **Adoption is not optional** ([20:26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=1226s)) - Like buying computers, the AI is an org investment; the biggest naysayers became her best advocates.
- **All data is good data** ([21:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=1291s)) - Transparency dashboards were resisted as intrusive, but she's never fired anyone over them — the point is a culture change.
- **Dashboards are only as good as the interpreter** ([22:25](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=1345s)) - She wrote narratives on top of charts for years; AI now automates that so managers can ask "what does this mean?"
- **AI wraps smarts around the data** ([25:26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=1526s)) - It's an interpretation layer for leadership, not a bot running the clinic.
- **Convert data into the audience's language** ([28:36](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=1716s)) - Social workers don't care about "productivity"; they care about helping more kids.
- **People want to be good at their job** ([31:14](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=1874s)) - AI gives autonomy and spares people the embarrassment of asking how to read a dashboard.
- **A data council across five states** ([32:01](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=1921s)) - Make An Impact convenes 15 members to define clinical use cases and demo what dashboards could become.
- **Lead with the business case, not the AI case** ([39:42](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=2382s)) - The takeaway: dialogue over decree, and frame change around outcomes.
- **Building a benchmarking platform** ([43:36](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIWGij3Z4Oo&t=2616s)) - 711 purpose-driven orgs in 25 states; the goal is sector-wide benchmarking that has never existed.
