# Product Is More Than Prompts

## Summary

In this episode of the Aboard Podcast, Paul Ford and Rich Ziade discuss how the rise of AI and "vibe coding" is disrupting the traditional roles of product management and design. Rich defines the ideal product manager as a "three-legged stool" balancing design usability, business objectives, and engineering constraints. However, the hosts argue that the current AI boom has allowed the engineering "leg" to dominate the conversation, often at the expense of user experience and business context. While AI allows for the rapid shipping of software, the results frequently reflect the underlying data models rather than intuitive tools designed for human needs.

The hosts predict that this engineering-centric phase is temporary and that the "stock" of product managers and designers will soon rise again. They suggest that the traditional role is evolving into a four-pronged discipline, where the professional must also act as a "diplomat" to AI-based systems. Ford warns that as "ship risk" disappears and tools become accessible to everyone -- including executives -- process and strategic alignment will become more critical than ever. Their primary advice for product professionals is to lead with their core disciplines of empathy and business strategy while learning enough about the technical landscape to effectively interrogate and guide AI-driven development.
