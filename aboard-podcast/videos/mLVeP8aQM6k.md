# Expertise Matters More Than Ever | The Aboard Podcast 2026-03-17

## Summary

Paul Ford and Rich Ziade tackle the thorny question of how to price professional services when AI has collapsed the time it takes to deliver work. Rich tells the story of a client engagement where Paul vibe-coded a fully functional data explorer in two hours -- work that would have been a $30,000-$40,000 proposal just five years ago. The client was intrigued but confused: if the prototype already exists, what exactly are they paying for? Rich admits he had no idea how to price it, and the two hosts wrestle with the implications in real time.

The conversation broadens into a wide-ranging discussion about the death of billable hours across professional services -- not just software, but law, accounting, and consulting. They compare AI's disruption to the transition from propeller planes to jets: the shift is happening too fast for people to process emotionally, even if the economics are clear. Paul argues that architecture and deep expertise remain genuinely hard, while the prototyping and scaffolding work that used to justify large bills has been commoditized overnight. Rich points to a lawyer named Zack Shapiro who runs a subscription-based law practice as a model for the future: $1,000 a month for ongoing legal services, enabled by AI tools that let two people serve 100 clients.

The hosts conclude that the future lies in the "middle" -- not the DIY crowd who will hack things together in Claude Code, and not the enterprise buyers who want massive custom implementations, but the organizations that need real expertise paired with AI-accelerated delivery. The key insight: relationships and trust matter more than ever precisely because the technical work has gotten cheaper. Clients are paying for a dedicated team, judgment, and the ability to evolve software over time -- not for hours logged.
