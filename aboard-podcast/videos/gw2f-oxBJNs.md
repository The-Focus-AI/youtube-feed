# The New Yorker's Andrew Marantz: What's Wrong With Sam Altman?

## Summary

Andrew Marantz, New Yorker staff writer and co-author (with Ronan Farrow) of the magazine's April 13 future-issue feature on Sam Altman, joins Paul and Rich to unpack 18 months of reporting on the OpenAI CEO. There is no smoking gun. What Marantz and Farrow assembled instead is a slow accumulation of documents and interviews — Ilya Sutskever's binders, emails from before OpenAI was founded in 2015, Y Combinator-era allegations Altman still denies, and small "kind of almost like inconsequential" details (a board "no" vote that ends up recorded as an abstention) that point to a "meticulous drive to kind of tidy up narrative loose ends." Marantz's framing question, which he says is structural rather than personal: why are we even asking about the personality flaws of an individual with this much power? Altman's own pitch — "I alone can be in charge of the Ring of Sauron because I have this kind of quality of being uncorruptible" — answers the question on its own.

The most distinctive piece of theory Marantz brings is the "Move 37" framing, which got cut from the published article for ballast. AlphaGo's move against Lee Sedol was so out of distribution that commentators thought the model had glitched out — and only worked out in retrospect. Some people who watched Altman across the OpenAI decade think his eight years of "please regulate me / this technology might kill everyone" rhetoric was his Move 37: a play so unconventional, hitting a Congress that was tired of giving tech CEOs carte blanche, a public burned by social media, and a recruitment pool of safety-pilled researchers, that no one could parse it until it was too late. Paul prefers an "Occam's razor of nerd incompetence" reading — the deals just kept showing up and he kept taking them. Rich offers the structural answer: this technology scaled past the Valley's own limits so fast that even Y Combinator's playbook broke down.

The conversation digs into specifics from the piece — the Pentagon blacklist Altman swooped on to compete with Anthropic, the post-firing "Godfather final scene" cleanup, Emirati Sheikhs and Saudi sovereign wealth as the only entities besides the US government and four tech giants that can fund this level of growth, gifted cars, the fact that everyone in this orbit keeps Google Docs like 19th-century Russian princesses keep diaries. Marantz's personal sentiment lands on the AI doomer end with caveats: he went in expecting to find a logical seam he could pull at and instead came out unable to refute the gravest arguments offhand. Rich asks if Musk is the good guy here — no — and Paul tries to close on "Altman will be part of our lives for 40 years," to which Marantz deadpans: "There are lots of people working there who don't think we have 40 years left, so that's encouraging in a way."

## Key Points

- **18 months of reporting** ([3:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gw2f-oxBJNs&t=195s)) - Marantz and Ronan Farrow each came at Altman from different angles (AI doomer reporting vs. Silicon Valley power reporting) and converged on him as the central figure.
- **The AI doomer paradox** ([3:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gw2f-oxBJNs&t=195s)) - "Why do so many of the people who are closest to building AI find it to be an existential risk to themselves and everyone they love and if so why do they keep building it?"
- **The November 2022 ChatGPT interview** ([4:51](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gw2f-oxBJNs&t=291s)) - Marantz interviewed ChatGPT for the New Yorker interviews issue; this is when he started thinking about Altman's public AI safety rhetoric.
- **Alan Kay's "there he is, the future"** ([6:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gw2f-oxBJNs&t=360s)) - Paul's specific memory of Alan Kay calling out Altman as the Valley's future when he was first taking over Y Combinator.
- **Charm via mirroring** ([8:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gw2f-oxBJNs&t=507s)) - Altman's superpower is convincing every camp (VCs, pure-tech innovators, AI doomers) that he is at bottom one of them.
- **Y Combinator question** ([10:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gw2f-oxBJNs&t=630s)) - Reporting suggests he was pushed out of the top job at YC even though he denies it.
- **The "no" vote recorded as abstention** ([10:51](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gw2f-oxBJNs&t=651s)) - On nonprofit-to-for-profit valuation, a board member said no but the official minutes recorded abstention.

- **Google Docs in anonymous mode** ([13:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gw2f-oxBJNs&t=826s)) - Rich's read: this is a culture that loves putting secrets into shareable Google Docs.
