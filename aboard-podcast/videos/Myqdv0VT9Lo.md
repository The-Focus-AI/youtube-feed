# John Herrman: The Declawing of OpenClaw

## Summary

New York Magazine tech columnist John Herrman joins Paul Ford and Rich Ziade to autopsy OpenClaw — the open-source "harness" (first known as a misspelled Claude-Bot) that vibe-coder Peter Steinberger built to give an AI model total access to your personal computer, files, inbox, and credentials, then let you text it like an executive assistant. For about three weeks it was the only thing anyone in the "AI sauna" on Twitter could talk about: Andrej Karpathy called it takeoff, Sam Altman anointed it the real thing (as opposed to the Moltbook fad), Jensen Huang called it the biggest thing since ChatGPT, and Mark Zuckerberg briefly got obsessed. Then it evaporated. Herrman, who reviewed it for his piece "My Adventures With 'The AI That Actually Does Things,'" reports that by the time he was writing, "the wave had already crashed" — the setup failed five times at every major step, and he got "negative value out of it."

The conversation pulls back to the structural problem underneath: OpenClaw was an engineer's toy that never got the approachable, Hotmail-style front door that turns niche tech into mainstream tech. Herrman and the hosts trace the familiar pattern — RealPlayer before YouTube, Kozmo before delivery apps, Broadcast.com before streaming — where the first clumsy version dies and the second go-round finds its footing. They dwell on how genuinely hard it is to write about AI (you end up "reviewing conversations"), why Herrman is bored to death of the subject, and his diversion into "The Mystery of 'Human Meat' for Sale on Temu" (a Hormel corned-beef-hash listing mangled by automated ad copy).

They close on Herrman's grounded predictions: one or more of these firms probably does become Google-scale and entrenched — but the payoff is that AI recedes into a "grinding enterprise software story" and gets boring, which is exactly what Paul and Rich (who run an AI transformation shop) are rooting for. Herrman warns against the moralistic strain of AI criticism that expects failure to deliver justice, and Paul predicts people will get used to "the machine God in like five minutes and start complaining about it." The episode ends in the darker territory Herrman actually enjoys: the "ecological collapse" of social platforms into liminal, slop-filled spaces where bots like the ones on Moltbook to talk to themselves in emptied-out digital malls.

## Key Points

- **John's background** ([1:56](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=116s)) - Six or seven years at the New York Times; The Awl, BuzzFeed, Gizmodo, Popular Mechanics — "basically the same thing in different fonts for a long time." Started covering Asian news wires and gadget releases around the iPhone.
- **AI's professional "firewall"** ([3:05](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=185s)) - Journalism is extraordinarily exposed to AI (text went "from very cheap to basically free"), which creates high resistance in the profession: "if you're all in on AI as a journalist, you're kind of done."
- **What OpenClaw is** ([5:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=329s)) - Peter Steinberger vibe-coded a "harness" that first went viral as a misspelled Claude-Bot; runs on a Mac Mini, connects to a model of your choice, and just does things.
- **Breaking every security best practice** ([6:08](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=368s)) - You hand it credentials and high-level access to your machine and accounts; "you're kind of hacking yourself."
- **Skipping a step** ([7:16](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=436s)) - Unlike sandboxed ChatGPT, OpenClaw lives on your computer, reads all your past email, and could "go straight to running your whole life."
- **You give it a name** ([8:52](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=532s)) - People personified it as a 24/7 executive assistant; Paul's was "Clyde."
- **Moltbook** ([10:06](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=606s)) - The social platform for AI agents plotting, scheming, and starting religions was OpenClaw underneath — people deploying their own agents.
- **The hype consensus** ([10:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=635s)) - Karpathy, Sam Altman, Jensen Huang, and briefly Mark Zuckerberg all pointed at it; Altman drew a line: Moltbook maybe a fad, but "OpenClaw, that's the real thing."
- **Everybody turned out to be wrong** ([11:37](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=697s)) - Elements got absorbed into the platforms (OpenAI hired the OpenClaw guy), but the "you take control" excitement faded.
- **Defining "harness"** ([14:02](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=842s)) - A software framework (like Claude Code) that provides an environment and tools to point a model toward a goal; OpenClaw was a "janky local version" focused on email and calendaring.
- **Never built for the masses** ([15:05](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=905s)) - Setup ran through a terminal and failed repeatedly; "this was never going to take off if it was just made for the nerds."
- **The Hotmail analogy** ([15:22](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=922s)) - Rich expected a big host to ship a simple web UI, the way Hotmail removed the need to run your own SMTP server.
- **The wave already crashed** ([16:47](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=1007s)) - By the time John was writing, it was fading — even a setup consultant was already "moving on to some other thing."
- **Gary Tan's fantasy config files** ([18:54](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=1134s)) - The Y Combinator head posted "second brain" configs that "read like fantasies of the best product in the world."
- **Reviewing conversations** ([21:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=1275s)) - The interface makes AI reviews hard: "you're ultimately reviewing conversations," and pasting transcripts is not thinking.
- **Troubleshooting IS the story** ([21:48](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=1308s)) - John spent his time asking "the smarter text box that I pay for" why the setup failed, then pretending he was still in control.
- **The receipt-in-downloads use case** ([24:53](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=1493s)) - The appeal was texting your bot via Telegram or iMessage to act on your local files.
- **Second-go-round finds its footing** ([25:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=1531s)) - Like Kozmo, RealPlayer, and YouTube — fast-moving tech stumbles out clumsily, then a wider audience arrives on the next attempt.
- **Engineers run the show** ([26:33](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=1593s)) - Approachability is "nowhere to be found"; designers are nervous about Claude Design, but UX will be "absolutely key down the road."
- **Human Meat on Temu** ([29:04](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=1744s)) - A Hormel corned-beef-hash listing labeled "Human Meat," likely from automated ad copy that muddled the pet-food vs. human-food distinction.
- **Bored to death** ([31:13](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=1873s)) - John admits the beat exhausts him; you're "writing about writing," positioning yourself against Sam Altman's story and already losing.
- **Coding really did change** ([32:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=1952s)) - Paul: enterprise coding is permanently altered — exciting "in a boring way" — but making a genuinely good product is not radically easier.
- **The productized-model curse** ([39:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=2340s)) - Once a thing gets named and becomes a subject of massive attention, "nine times out of ten" it's doomed — it becomes a target for the labs.
- **The Facebook lesson** ([41:26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=2486s)) - A rotting feed didn't mean Meta was finished; things "go further than you expect and never quite in the way they were talked about."
- **Moral criticism doesn't land on winners** ([42:36](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=2556s)) - Tales with a moral component always hit a "second-tier player" without the tailwind of global capital, not the Sam Altmans.
- **Ecological collapse of social media** ([47:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Myqdv0VT9Lo&t=2851s)) - John's real interest: platforms becoming "liminal spaces," emptied-out digital malls where slop is liked by slop.
