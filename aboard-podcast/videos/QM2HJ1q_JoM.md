# Challenge: Don't Mention AI

## Summary

After fielding internal pleas from their team to talk about literally anything else, Paul Ford and Rich Ziade attempt a 20-minute "no AI" experiment on the Aboard Podcast. They mostly succeed. Rich opens with Jony Ive's new Ferrari design — an electric, powder-blue, "upside-down bathtub" shape that has set off a wave of meme outrage from the brand's traditional sports-car base. From there they spiral into the real story of the show: online sports gambling. Rich, who watches the Yankees on the Gotham Sports app, describes a screen that's two-thirds yes/no betting prompts and one-third actual game. They argue that America rolled out gambling "like blockchain" — quietly, with no public debate — and the country now needs a moment of bravery to put it back in the box.

Paul follows with two parenting-driven riffs. First, he asked an LLM to recommend "smart shows" for his pre-teen daughter and realized the AI was rummaging through the same Netflix algorithm she already swims in: a narrow, sealed cultural smoothie where everyone gets the same dozen shows. He uses this to argue that America used to be much, much weirder — Oneida communities, Shakers, Slayer shirts under church suits, Megadeth recommendations in junior high — and that escaping the feed is the actual hard work of culture now. Then he pivots to The Maintainers (themaintainers.org), a community focused on repair and longevity over disposability, prompted by his own daughter learning to sew with his wife. Rich shares his own attempts to learn lead-free soldering to fix a busted headphone jack on a synthesizer.

They close on optimism: the Virtual OS Museum (a 150-gigabyte torrent of 570 operating systems, curated by Andrew Warkentin) as Christmas for retrocomputing nerds; the relaunched Commodore 64 his friend just ordered; and a defense of the open web — "anybody can go publish any web page they want any time. They never took that away from you." Paul notes the audience boos at commencement speeches that tell graduates to "get ready for AI" and reads that defiance as healthy. Rich asks listeners to write in about something they're doing that doesn't involve a prompt.
