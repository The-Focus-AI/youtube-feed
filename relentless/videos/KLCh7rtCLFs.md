# #40 - WTF is App Mafia - 18/yo earning $45M/yr building apps

## Summary

Ti Morse sits down in Miami with the four co-founders of App Mafia -- Zach Yadegari, Blake Anderson, Alex Slater, and Connor McLarenn -- young app developers collectively responsible for over 100 million downloads and tens of millions in annual revenue. The group met in Vegas at a mobile app convention and decided to live together in Miami, where they built a collective focused on scaling consumer apps through aggressive social media marketing and controversial content strategies. They are launching a course teaching their app-building playbook, deliberately courting controversy because they believe sacrificing social capital for actual capital is a winning trade.

The conversation covers their approach to "winning with controversy," hyper-scaling through a MrBeast promotion (spending $500K on a single ad), reinvesting everything back into the business, the technical stack behind their app Quittr, reducing cognitive load as a design philosophy, and bootstrapping super-profitable apps. They discuss the controversy around Cal AI's alleged revenue faking, the concept of "letting little fires burn so you can focus on the biggest fire," forcing timelines to move faster, and their wildly ambitious future plans. The episode includes colorful tangents about Alex lying about being a Thiel Fellow, Nik Shevchenko hiring gay strippers using Thiel Fellowship money, Roy Lee's ambition to become "the next Genghis Khan," studying complex systems, "bad girlfriend retention" as a metaphor for app retention, and Zach's three-year plan to build a nuclear warhead. This is one of the most chaotic and entertaining episodes of the podcast, showcasing the unapologetically aggressive mindset of young app entrepreneurs.
