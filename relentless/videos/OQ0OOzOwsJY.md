# How to Start a Cult | Lulu Cheng Meservey

## Summary

Ti Morse sits down with communications strategist Lulu Cheng Meservey, founder of Rostra, to unpack the mechanics of building a founder-led "cult" — the kind of high-conviction culture that lets startups recruit unreasonable people for unreasonable missions. Lulu argues every cult needs a cult leader, and the only way to recruit the first believers is to sit across from someone, look like a madman, and swear on your life the impossible thing can be done. She distinguishes between traditional charisma (Trump, Palmer Luckey), quiet substance (Trae Stephens), and high-integrity trustworthiness (Scott Wu), and insists the only sustainable strategy is "be yourself, but more" — letting the magnet collect its filings over time.

The middle of the conversation focuses on culture maintenance: how to choose who to alienate, why personal loyalty beats NDAs, why "leaders eat last" should literally mean eating in the same room at the same time, and how to resist "salami slicing" — the small encroachments by employees, activists, or external actors that compound into catastrophic constraints (the Google example). Lulu walks through deterrence as incentive-shaping, the value of overreacting early so you never have to overreact later, and uses Napoleon's "Les Hommes sans Peur" to show how naming a thing changes who will volunteer for it.

The last third moves into the craft of public-facing storytelling — the three levels of a company story (what you do, what it represents, the dent in the universe), why distribution is just the plating on a dish that has to be good underneath, and why you should pursue *auctoritas* (Cicero, Roman moral authority with an iron core) rather than chase fleeting *aura*. Along the way: Jensen Huang's "data center in a box," Willy Brandt's genuflection in Warsaw, JP Morgan toilet-paper signs that people actually paid into, and Cicero putting the jury itself on trial.

## Key Points

- **Make a terrorist group "acephalous"** ([0:26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=26s)) — Cut off the head. By analogy, the founder is the head of the cult and the magnet for slings and arrows.
- **The first few followers are the hardest** ([0:26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=26s)) — After ~10, the people recruit the people. Before then, you have to sound like a madman and swear on your life.
- **Three kinds of charisma** ([3:05](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=185s)) — Traditional (Palmer, Trump), quiet substantive (Trae Stephens), high-integrity / trustworthy (Scott Wu).
- **Be yourself, but more** ([5:08](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=308s)) — You can't lie to everyone forever. Unleash yourself and the magnet gradually collects its filings.
- **Chaotic = what people call you when they don't understand** ([8:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=510s)) — Use the "Domino's pizza tracker" to keep stakeholders oriented to the destination during the rubble phase.
- **Cull the herd early — opt-in to hardcore** ([11:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=690s)) — Elon flipped the X email from opt-out severance to opt-in hardcore; getting rid of "deep state" employees compounds.
- **Going direct: judge the net, not one tweet** ([12:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=720s)) — You can't build an audience without errors. Bake them in.
- **Language is shorthand that creates reality** ([13:58](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=838s)) — Napoleon's "Les Hommes sans Peur" replaced the literal pitch ("come get shot at this cannon") with an identity volunteers wanted.
- **Pick whose side you're not on** ([19:24](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=1164s)) — Avoiding a side gets you loved by nobody. Anduril chose to be on the side of people in uniform.
- **The most catastrophic crises are internal** ([20:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=1200s)) — Brian Armstrong cut internal attackers and was fine. Internal sabotage kills companies; external attacks rarely do.
- **Fight for someone else, not yourself** ([21:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=1260s)) — Self-defense reads as self-interested. Advocate for someone with aligned interests and it lands harder.
- **Deterrence is incentive shaping** ([23:22](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=1402s)) — As exposure grows, more people take potshots. Don't pursue temporary pain avoidance.
- **Salami slicing — Google's activist problem** ([25:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=1531s)) — Individual instances seem too small to act on; the compounded result handcuffs you out of military/government work.
- **Overreact early so you don't have to overreact later** ([27:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=1620s)) — You're reacting to the 100 things that would have happened, not the one.
- **Stopping leaks requires tyrant mode** ([29:34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=1774s)) — Like weeding Al-Qaeda from a village. Prevention is better, and prevention = personal loyalty to the founder.
- **NDAs are LARP** ([30:20](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=1820s)) — If the paper is what's protecting you, something has already gone wrong.
- **Loyalty: Nigerian-prince scheme for goodwill** ([32:39](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=1959s)) — Put small meaningful acts of goodwill out, see who reciprocates, engage further with those.
- **Leaders eat last — literally, in the same room** ([33:45](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=2025s)) — Scott Wu eating in the lunchroom, Napoleon among the troops, Shackleton refusing easier shifts.
- **Visuals beat specs** ([36:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=2160s)) — Willy Brandt's genuflection in Warsaw, Steve Jobs and the manila envelope MacBook Air.
- **Jensen invented "time to train AlexNet"** ([37:33](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=2253s)) — A made-up metric, plus "data center in a box," that beat any spec sheet.
- **People root for what you represent** ([39:58](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=2398s)) — Not for you the person — for the thing in the world you stand for and for what they see of themselves in you.
- **Recruit on the spirit, not the letter** ([42:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=2535s)) — JFK's NASA janitor "helping us get to the moon." Startup work is objectively worse than being a janitor unless the spirit is real.
- **Three levels of story** ([43:18](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=2598s)) — What you do / what it represents / the dent in the universe. A-plus-plus talent only shows up for level three.
- **Distribution is the plating** ([45:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=2700s)) — Founders are obsessed with reach, but nobody asks "what are we trying to say?" Good plating can't save a bad dish.
- **The secret truth most people assassinate** ([51:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=3060s)) — Almost everyone has a special truth inside them and kills it because others don't seem to share it.
- **JP Morgan toilet-paper signs** ([52:34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=3154s)) — Lulu's experiment showed humans have a pro-social default to obey signage — which is also the lever you use to lead a moral quarter-turn.
- **Cicero put the jury on trial** ([57:28](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=3448s)) — When all incentives ran the wrong way, he flipped the frame: "you're the ones on trial," and the jury voted for itself.
- **Offense = small surface area, max pressure** ([58:56](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=3536s)) — Attack one specific thing whose truth unravels everything else (P = F/A in narrative form).
- **Defense = spread the surface area** ([60:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=3641s)) — Reframe the attack as an attack on what you represent, so others have to defend you to defend themselves.
- **Auctoritas over aura** ([64:16](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQ0OOzOwsJY&t=3856s)) — Aura is fleeting and event-bound; auctoritas is durable authority with an iron moral core.
