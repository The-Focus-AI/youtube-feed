# #33 - Launching Mirrors Into Space | Ben Nowack, CEO Reflect Orbital

## Summary

Ti Morse interviews Ben Nowack, CEO and co-founder of Reflect Orbital, a company putting mirrors in space to sell sunlight after dark. Ben grew up on Cape Cod using a table saw at age five, building RC planes, fusion reactors, and bombs as a teenager. His YouTube channel "Ben Builds" documented his maker projects and ultimately landed him a job at SpaceX, where he worked on Dragon 2 propulsion tanks and rocket engines. After SpaceX, Ben spent years iterating on company ideas, stack-ranking them by potential impact, before landing on the concept of space mirrors -- inspired by the Russian Znamya experiments that used orbital mirrors to illuminate Siberian mines.

The core insight behind Reflect Orbital is elegant: the sun produces orders of magnitude more energy than humanity uses, and mirrors are extraordinarily cheap per square meter. By launching lightweight mirrors into orbit, Reflect can redirect sunlight to solar farms after sunset, effectively extending the solar day. The cost of getting mass to orbit has dropped 10x since 2010, and mirror material costs roughly $2 per square meter, making the economics increasingly viable. The company received 260,000 applications for sunlight from 157 countries after going viral. Ben discusses the hot air balloon test that validated the concept, the satellite launch timeline, the "idiot index" for space parts (the ratio of what you pay vs. raw material cost), hiring A+ talent, and his personal struggle with depression during college and Lyme disease. He emphasizes that finding an idea worth spending your whole life on is the hardest and most important part of entrepreneurship.
