# #36 - Solving Health Using AI | Max Marchione, Superpower

## Summary

Ti Morse interviews Max Marchione, co-founder of Superpower, a company using AI to democratize access to healthcare. The conversation opens with Max describing his team's Friday ritual of injecting each other with peptides -- Thymosin Alpha 1 for immune defense, NAD+ for cellular energy, and Retatrutide (a "GLP-3" triple agonist). Max's philosophy is that everything he does that people laugh at and call crazy eventually becomes mainstream, and he wants to live in the future by experimenting with cutting-edge health interventions.

The interview covers an extraordinarily wide range of topics: Max's sleep optimization stack, the risks of blindly following AI health advice, his controversial views on nicotine as a cognitive enhancer, building a "pro autism stack," learning to dream bigger from Masayoshi Son (Masa) of SoftBank, and his framework of "$100B company in 10 years, $1B company in 3 years, $10M revenue in 1 year." Max explains why most brands in the world are "3 out of 10" quality, why talent is exponentially distributed (the best person is not twice as good but 100x), how to train intuition, and the US healthcare system's perverse incentive structures. He also discusses gene editing possibilities like sleeping only 5 hours a night, the importance of obsessing over revenue, and pushing through when nobody believes in you. The conversation touches on Max's early businesses and his long-term vision of building a city.
