# #34 - Bringing Manufacturing Back To America | Aaron Slodov, CEO Atomic Industries

## Summary

Ti Morse interviews Aaron Slodov, founder of Atomic Industries and co-founder of Reindustrialize, for the second time on the podcast. Aaron's central thesis is that American manufacturing skill and tribal knowledge has been evaporating for decades, and the consequences are becoming dangerously visible -- exemplified by the fact that the US military put out a public request to rebuild a mold for the B-2 Bomber because the original designers are gone and the drawings no longer exist. Atomic Industries uses AI to transform legacy manufacturing operations, while Reindustrialize is a grassroots movement and conference series aimed at educating people across the manufacturing stack about what re-industrialization actually looks like in practice.

The conversation covers why Aaron spent a full year cold-calling over 200 machine shops across America (while pretending to be a Stanford Business School student doing a semester project), how manufacturing startup financing differs fundamentally from software venture capital, and why the SpaceX/Anduril model of massive venture-backed hardware companies should not be treated as a one-of-a-kind anomaly but rather replicated more broadly. Aaron argues that there are enormous opportunities in multi-billion dollar manufacturing verticals that are ripe for technological transformation. He emphasizes that the hard part is not the technology itself but building the conviction to work on a decades-long problem while the rest of the market chases software-like returns. The episode ends with a lighter personal note about the logistical challenges of caring for his dog who needs surgery.
