# Anthropic's $30B Ramp, Mythos Doomsday, OpenClaw Ankled, Iran War Ceasefire, Israel's Influence

## Summary

Friedberg is officially "out this week" and Brad Gerstner — labeled in the JSON as David Friedberg — joins the besties as guest. The cold open is pure All-In: Chamath asks how many PRs will get pushed to the "core structural internet" in 100 days, then immediately pivots to dick pics and Balaji videos. The first big topic is Anthropic withholding Mythos (or Mithos in the Greek pronunciation), claiming the model is too dangerous to release. Their published study includes a 27-year-old OpenBSD vulnerability and 16-year-old bugs in widely deployed firewall code. Sacks gives them benefit of the doubt this time after calling the previous Anthropic blackmail study "embarrassing to the level of being a hoax." Chamath calls Mythos's framing "mostly theater," noting that in February 2019 the same Dario-led team at OpenAI did this with the 1.5B-parameter GPT-2.

The conversation moves to OpenAI's Spud — the first Blackwell-trained model, "any day now" — and the broader compute race. Brad/Friedberg pulls the besties to "peak OpenAI FUD" and credits Anthropic's $30B run-rate Codex ramp. Chamath stress-tests the cycle by tracking the language: "When you can't talk about EBITDA you talk about revenue, and when you can't talk about revenue you talk about gross revenue. We're between gross and net revenue." On agents, Chamath argues coding wins agents because agents need to write code, so the coding-token leader compounds into the agent-leader. The discussion turns to Macrohard (Sacks's running Microsoft joke), Apple/Amazon's "less retarded" Alexa and Siri refreshes, and the OpenClaw/Peter Steinberger drama where the open-source Anthropic-replacement got "ankled" by overnight pricing changes.

The final third covers the New York Times "inside the room" piece on the Iran war's run-up and the question of Israel's influence on US foreign policy. Sacks flags Naftali Bennett tweeting concern about declining Israel-US polling. Jason praises X's auto-translate as "the most impressive tech feature released in years." Sacks closes with the X efficiency point — Elon's company has 70% fewer employees than the day he walked in — and uses it as a bullet point for the "peak employment in tech" thesis. The 5th annual All-In Summit in LA gets a plug, Sacks is "advised that I can attend business in the state for business reasons," and the boys debate paying to claim their real names on X.
