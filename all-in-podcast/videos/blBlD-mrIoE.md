# How Matt Mahan Thinks He Can Save California

## Summary

San Jose Mayor and California gubernatorial candidate Matt Mahan joins all four besties for an in-depth interview on his plan to fix the Golden State. Mahan presents a damning diagnosis: California has increased state spending by 75% over six years -- $150 billion more annually -- while outcomes in crime, homelessness, housing, and education have remained flat or declined. He argues this is not a money problem but an "incentives problem" where a sprawling bureaucracy, public sector unions, and a cottage industry of consultants and litigators consume resources without accountability for results.

The conversation drills deep into California's housing crisis, where Mahan reveals that one-time impact fees can add 20% to project costs and anyone -- even non-residents -- can sue under CEQA to block development. He contrasts California's $1 million-per-unit homeless housing with San Jose's $85,000 "sleeping cabins" that achieve better outcomes. On energy, he criticizes policies that have given California the highest gas prices and electricity costs in the continental U.S. while simultaneously driving up insurance costs to the point where State Farm has left the market entirely.

The interview also covers the $1 trillion pension time bomb (CalPERS and CalSTRS combined), California's $35 billion budget deficit despite record-high tax rates, the proposed state wealth tax that Mahan argues would trigger massive capital flight, and his approach to immigration and federal relations under the Trump administration. Throughout, Mahan advocates for "zero-based budgeting," performance dashboards, and the willingness to say "no" to Sacramento special interests -- positioning himself as a pragmatic, results-oriented alternative in the gubernatorial race.
