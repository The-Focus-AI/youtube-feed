# They're Opening the Stock Market to Everyone. Here's What That Actually Means

## Summary

SEC Chair Paul Atkins and CFTC Chair Michael Selig join Jason Calacanis and Chamath Palihapitiya for a wide-ranging interview on the future of American capital markets. The conversation opens with Atkins drawing a stark contrast between the 1980s, when companies like Apple and Microsoft went public early and public investors captured most of the returns, and today, where the U.S. has half the public companies it had 30 years ago and the majority of gains go to insiders, private equity, and venture capital. Both chairs outline an aggressive agenda to reverse this trend by cutting compliance costs, reducing litigation risk, and reforming corporate governance rules that have made the IPO process prohibitively burdensome.

A central theme is the historic cooperation between the SEC and CFTC, two agencies that Atkins describes as having operated like "two fortresses with no man's land in between." The chairs announce they are drafting a memorandum of understanding to harmonize their regulatory frameworks, particularly for cross-jurisdictional products like crypto assets and prediction markets. Selig emphasizes the need for "purpose-fit" rules rather than forcing blockchain projects into decades-old forms like the S-1, while Atkins envisions a "super-app" approach that blurs jurisdictional lines for the benefit of market participants.

The interview also tackles the accredited investor standard, venture capital fund formation, the potential move from quarterly to semi-annual reporting, AI-driven autonomous trading agents, prediction market integrity, and the growing concern about gambling addiction among young men. Both chairs stress that while innovation must be fostered onshore rather than driven to offshore jurisdictions, robust fraud enforcement and investor protections remain non-negotiable -- pointing to the FTX collapse as a cautionary tale and the CFTC-supervised LedgerX as proof that proper regulation works.

## Key Points

- **IPO market has collapsed** ([1:04](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=64s)) - The U.S. has half the number of public companies as 30 years ago; investment returns have shifted from public investors to insiders, PE, and VC
- **Three inhibitions to going public** ([5:07](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=307s)) - Cost of compliance rules, threat of class action litigation, and weaponization of corporate governance through shareholder proposals
- **CFTC priorities for 2026** ([6:34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=394s)) - Purpose-fit rules for crypto, prediction markets, and AI; ending regulation by enforcement from the prior administration
- **AI trading agents raising systemic risk questions** ([8:22](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=502s)) - Young projects are building automated agent-based hedge funds that replace traditional firms like Citadel and Millennium
- **T-zero settlement via distributed ledger** ([10:42](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=642s)) - SEC sees immediate delivery-versus-payment on blockchain as an exciting near-term prospect, though speed bumps may be needed
- **SEC-CFTC harmonization via MOU** ([16:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=1006s)) - The agencies are drafting a memorandum of understanding to share information and end jurisdictional turf battles
- **"Super-app" regulatory vision** ([18:36](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=1116s)) - Atkins envisions blurred lines between agencies with coordinated, low-friction oversight for dually registered companies
- **Prediction markets need integrity controls** ([21:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=1290s)) - Exchanges must certify contracts are not susceptible to insider trading or manipulation; Kalshi has already brought enforcement actions
- **Markets as "truth machines"** ([23:18](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=1398s)) - Prediction markets proved more accurate than polls in the 2024 election; keeping them onshore prevents foreign disinformation
- **Quarterly reporting may change** ([28:03](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=1683s)) - SEC will propose a rule on reporting cadence; quarterly reporting only began in 1970 and the UK reverted to semi-annual in 2014
- **Accredited investor reform coming in 2026** ([32:38](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=1958s)) - Atkins commits to tackling the wealth-based standard and exploring a sophistication test like a driver's license for investing
- **VC fund formation limits hurt participation** ([40:37](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=2437s)) - Chamath was capped at 100 investors despite $100M+ in demand; statutory fund limits restrict democratization
- **Opening 401k plans to private markets** ([42:54](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=2574s)) - SEC working with Department of Labor and Treasury to allow private investments in retirement plans with proper guardrails
- **Self-certification vs. SEC approval** ([39:14](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=2354s)) - Atkins envies the CFTC's streamlined self-certification process for new products; SEC's approach is far more labor-intensive
- **Separating capital raises from tokens** ([51:07](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=3067s)) - Selig argues tokens can be commodities, goods, or tools while the capital raise is a separate securities question
- **FTX vs. LedgerX as proof of regulation** ([54:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=3267s)) - The CFTC-supervised LedgerX survived the FTX implosion with no customer losses, proving proper oversight works
- **Gen Z gambling crisis** ([56:34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=el6ObB60Fb4&t=3394s)) - 45% of young men 18-30 report wagering problems, 10% meet addiction criteria; education and platform-level controls are key
