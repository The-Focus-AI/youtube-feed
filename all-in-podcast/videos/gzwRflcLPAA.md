# War with Iran + Pentagon vs Anthropic with Under Secretary of War Emil Michael

## Summary

In this emergency episode of the All-In Podcast (Episode 263), Jason Calacanis, Chamath Palihapitiya, David Friedberg, and guest Emil Michael -- the Under Secretary of War for Research and Engineering -- discuss two major topics: the U.S.-Israel joint military operation against Iran (Operation Epic Fury, day six) and the Pentagon's unprecedented decision to designate Anthropic as a supply chain risk.

On Iran, Emil Michael explains that President Trump described this as a "weeks, not months" operation aimed at disarming Iran's ability to supply terrorist groups like Hezbollah and Hamas, as well as eliminating nuclear and ICBM capabilities. Chamath presents a geopolitical thesis that the real strategic purpose is creating leverage with China ahead of April negotiations, since Iran and Venezuela supply approximately 20% of China's oil. Friedberg adds that a "grand bargain" with China could be the administration's crowning achievement. The discussion covers the revolutionary impact of drone warfare, AI-controlled autonomous systems, the discombobulator (a classified weapon used in Venezuela), and Israel's Iron Beam laser defense system. Emil emphasizes that new rules of engagement and a well-trained military -- combined with modern technology -- allow for swift, decisive operations without boots on the ground.

The second major segment covers the Pentagon's formal designation of Anthropic as a supply chain risk -- the first time this has happened to an American company. Emil reveals the inside story: after discovering that Anthropic's contract included a 20-page list of restrictions preventing the DoD from using Claude to plan kinetic strikes or move satellites, he spent three months negotiating for "all lawful use" terms. The breaking point came when an Anthropic executive called Palantir (the prime contractor) after the Venezuela raid to ask if their software was used -- raising alarms about reliability. All other major AI companies (OpenAI, Google, Grok) have agreed to "all lawful use" terms. Chamath warns that this episode reveals a broader risk for any organization relying on a single AI model, calling it "deplatforming times a thousand." The hosts debate Anthropic's valuation, with Friedberg arguing it could still be worth $1.5 trillion despite the controversy, while Emil suggests the supply chain risk designation has reduced its value.
