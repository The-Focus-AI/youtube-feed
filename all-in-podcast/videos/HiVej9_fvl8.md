# Iran War, Oil Shock, Off Ramps, AI's Revenue Explosion and PR Nightmare

## Summary

Brad Gerstner joins the besties as a guest host in place of David Friedberg for a wide-ranging episode covering the Iran war's economic fallout and AI's simultaneous revenue explosion and PR crisis. The group analyzes oil price volatility driven by the Strait of Hormuz disruption, with Chamath warning that the destruction of desalination plants could threaten drinking water for over 100 million people in the Gulf region. David Sacks argues the war polling poorly with American voters and warns against the neocon push for regime change, while Brad Gerstner notes that China's decision not to take sides creates a potential diplomatic off-ramp through Gulf state mediation.

The second half pivots to AI, where Jason reveals that Anthropic hit a $5-6 billion month in February and OpenAI is on a similar trajectory -- making them the fastest-scaling companies in business history. The group debates whether the massive J-curve of infrastructure spending (hundreds of billions in capex) will produce returns, with Sacks estimating break-even requires 5-6 years of compounding. The episode takes a sharp turn into AI's PR nightmare: 75% of Americans now believe AI risks outweigh benefits, 40% of proposed data centers have been cancelled due to local protests, and Stanford research shows anti-AI sentiment is being amplified by funded campaigns in major newsrooms.

The episode closes with Washington state's new "millionaire tax" driving Howard Schultz and other wealthy residents to Miami, which the besties use as a case study in how wealth taxes backfire. Chamath cites a Hoover Institution analysis showing California's proposed billionaire tax would destroy more revenue than it generates, while Brad draws parallels to the Gilded Age, when similar asset seizure proposals were ultimately abandoned in favor of the income tax system.
