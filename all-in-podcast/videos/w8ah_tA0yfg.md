# Socialists Sweep NYC, China Catches Up in Coding, AI Memory Crunch, Micron's Blowout Quarter

## Summary

Friedberg takes a "mental health day" after the socialist sweep in New York City, so the Besties bring in two guests — Travis Kalanick (fresh off announcing his AI startup Adams) and investor Gavin Baker (fresh off the SpaceX IPO). The first half dissects the DSA's clean sweep of NYC's Democratic congressional primaries: Mamdani-endorsed candidates went three-for-three, unseating strong incumbents like Dan Goldman and an ally of Hakeem Jeffries. Sacks reads out the DSA platform and a co-chair's quote about using the Democratic Party as "a ballot access vehicle," Chamath argues AI could be the antidote if Silicon Valley stopped being its own worst brand ambassador, and the group debates social-media bans for under-16s, the Israel issue's growing salience in Democratic primaries, and whether Mamdani is simply the most talented politician of his generation.

The second half pivots to the AI race. China's open-source GLM-5.2 (744B parameters, MIT license) has caught up to the previous generation of US frontier models on coding benchmarks — Gavin explains distillation via "Chinese iPhone farms" harvesting reasoning traces, argues the future is "composable models" with a router in front of a council of LLMs, and names Nvidia as America's real open-source champion. Sacks (former AI Czar) frames Anthropic's Fable rollback as self-inflicted regulatory capture, and Chamath is exasperated that China is "six months behind on the model and 24 months behind on the silicon, yet only a few months behind in total." Micron's blowout quarter (revenue up 4x, stock up 10x) anchors a discussion of the HBM/DRAM bottleneck now driving "AI inflation" into Apple, Xbox, and consumer hardware.

The final stretch runs on infrastructure and capital markets. Chamath lays out the first-principles math for orbital compute — "racks in space, linked with lasers" — where cheap reusable Starship launches could undercut the inflationary $25B of terrestrial power-and-cooling per gigawatt. The group riffs on Tesla's "Megapod" trademark, distributed inference pools using home Powerwalls and Starlinks, and the disaggregation of inference into prefill and decode (Groq/Cerebras). They close on the IPO wave: Gavin's $3 trillion valuation for Anthropic, the SpaceX float, Cerebras breaking deal price, and a nostalgic reminiscence about when Uber's $17B round felt enormous.

- **AI as the greatest economic leveler** ([3:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w8ah_tA0yfg&t=197s)) - Chamath argues AI could create the most equality in our lifetimes but has "very poor brand ambassadors," letting doomerism fill the vacuum.
- **The DSA platform** ([7:08](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w8ah_tA0yfg&t=428s)) - Sacks reads it out: abolish the Senate, ICE, prisons, the electoral college; free Palestine; public ownership of major corporations; defund the Department of War.

- **Truth and justice as society's immune system** ([15:01](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w8ah_tA0yfg&t=901s)) - Travis's aphorism: when the immune system is suppressed, social ills flare up.
- **Three socialist-leaning countries** ([24:04](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w8ah_tA0yfg&t=1444s)) - Chamath cites Canada, UK, Australia as cautionary tales — and their under-16 social media bans as a potential turning point.
- **The censorship angle on age-gating** ([27:11](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w8ah_tA0yfg&t=1631s)) - Travis counters Chamath: banning under-16s forces adults to de-anonymize, enabling a censorship regime.
- **80% of Democrats disapprove of Israel** ([33:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w8ah_tA0yfg&t=2009s)) - Sacks frames Dan Goldman's loss to Brad Lander as a near-straight vote on Israel.
- **Disambiguating Jews, Israelis, and Bibi** ([36:57](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w8ah_tA0yfg&t=2217s)) - Friedberg draws a 10/7-to-9/11 parallel and argues conflating one leader with an entire religion is insane.
- **GLM-5.2** ([45:12](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w8ah_tA0yfg&t=2712s)) - China's Z.AI ships a 744B-parameter, 1M-context, MIT-licensed open model scoring 51 on the Artificial Analysis index — trailing Opus 4.8 by under a point on SWE coding.
- **Composable models** ([48:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w8ah_tA0yfg&t=2901s)) - Gavin's thesis: a router sends most queries to your own RL'd open-weights model, with frontier models as the conductor checking the hardest ones.

- **OpenAI's "Jalapeño" chip** ([52:16](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w8ah_tA0yfg&t=3136s)) - Built with Broadcom; Gavin predicts Nvidia could retaliate by launching an OpenAI competitor.
- **Regulatory capture** ([55:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w8ah_tA0yfg&t=3317s)) - Sacks argues Dario got the "FAA for AI" he wanted but got hoisted on his own petard when Fable was rolled back.
