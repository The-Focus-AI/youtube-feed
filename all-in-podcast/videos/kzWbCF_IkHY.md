# Software Stocks Implode, Claude's Hit List, State of the Union Reactions, Trump's Tariff Pivot

## Summary

The besties tackle a packed episode covering the cascading impact of Anthropic's Claude on software stocks, the viral Citrini Research "2028 Global Intelligence Crisis" Substack post that tanked financial stocks, the State of the Union address, the science of reversing aging with Yamanaka factors, and the Supreme Court's landmark ruling striking down Trump's emergency tariff powers.

Chamath explains that the market has shifted from debating "when" AI will impact corporate cash flows to "if" those cash flows will survive at all, driving a massive repricing of SaaS and legacy tech stocks. Jason shares his hands-on experience using OpenClaw agents to automate knowledge work across his firm, building internal tools that replace both purchased software and human labor. Sacks argues that doomer narratives about AI-driven job loss are more appealing but less likely than abundance scenarios, citing Jevons' paradox and rising demand for software engineers. The discussion around data centers highlights growing local opposition that has already cost the industry an estimated $50 billion in cancelled projects, with Sacks describing the ratepayer protection pledge as the solution.

On the political front, the episode covers Trump's State of the Union address where Democrats refused to applaud on multiple consensus issues, Brad Gerstner's double shout-out from the president, and the Supreme Court's 6-3 ruling against Trump's emergency tariff powers. The besties debate whether this represents the system working as intended and what paths remain for the administration to pursue tariffs through alternative legal mechanisms. Friedberg delivers a science corner on the first-ever FDA-approved human trial of Yamanaka factors to reverse blindness, which could mark the beginning of anti-aging therapeutics.
