# Anthropic's Generational Run, OpenAI Panics, AI Moats, Meta Loses Major Lawsuits

## Summary

The full Fantastic Four are back as Sacks rolls in from DC just hours after being named co-chair of the President's Council of Advisors on Science and Technology (PCAST). The opening macro topic is Anthropic's astonishing year — Sacks credits MCP and the company's coding bet, while Chamath argues their non-political stance and 3,000-PhD culture are real strategic moats, not branding. Friedberg cuts through the press cycle by pointing out that Anthropic recognizes "gross tonnage" revenue while OpenAI is conservative, so the headline numbers people compare are not apples-to-apples.

The conversation pivots to OpenAI in apparent panic mode: chasing Anthropic into the enterprise, considering PE roll-ups for AI deployment, and reportedly walking back chunks of its Microsoft and licensing arrangements. Friedberg lays out a vision of consumer AI as the "meta-service" that 290M+ Spotify and 325M+ Netflix subscribers will gladly stack — and Chamath tags this as the third great wave he's lived through, "100x bigger" than the move to mobile and social. The crew unpacks the SaaS apocalypse, Apple's pivot to "value brands" like the rumored MacBook Neo, and what Chamath calls "strangulation as a service" — enterprises asking 8090 for shims that hide all SaaS UIs behind agentic flows.

The back half tackles personal-responsibility politics around Meta's twin lawsuit losses ($3M in LA, $375M in New Mexico for child-exploitation), parental controls vs. AI bans for teens, and Apple's value-brand cohort. They close with Sacks's PCAST roster — Marc Andreessen, Larry Ellison, Sergey, Michael Dell, Lisa Su, Jensen, Zuck, Friedberg — and the running joke that Friedberg is actually being elevated to the President's Council on Podcasting alongside Lex Fridman and Jason.
