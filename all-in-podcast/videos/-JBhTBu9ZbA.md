# Four CEOs on the Future of AI: CoreWeave, Perplexity, Mistral, and IREN

## Summary

Recorded at Nvidia's GTC conference, Jason Calacanis conducts four back-to-back interviews with AI infrastructure and application CEOs. CoreWeave CEO Michael Intrator traces his company's journey from an algorithmic hedge fund through crypto mining to becoming the premier AI cloud provider, explaining how donating A100 GPUs to the EleutherAI open source project became the "tuition" that taught CoreWeave to run scale parallelized computing. He pushes back on the GPU obsolescence narrative, arguing that older hardware retains significant commercial value across 5-6 year contract lifecycles, and reveals that constraints have shifted from chips to power, memory, storage, and networking.

Perplexity CEO Aravind Srinivas describes building the "answer engine" that has attracted over 100 million monthly active users by combining search, reasoning, and synthesis into a single product. He explains how Perplexity orchestrates multiple specialized models rather than building its own foundation model, and demonstrates how a task that would have taken a week-long project was completed by Perplexity's agentic system in minutes. Srinivas positions speed as Perplexity's primary moat, arguing that maintaining quality, speed, and trust simultaneously is something big companies fundamentally struggle to do.

Mistral CEO Arthur Mensch makes the case for open-source AI models as essential for enterprise adoption, arguing that companies need to protect intellectual property and deeply customize AI for specialized verticals. He announces a new partnership with Nvidia to build and open-source models optimized for Nvidia hardware. IREN CEO Daniel Roberts closes the episode by explaining how power has become the primary bottleneck for AI scaling, with his company building massive data centers in remote locations to tap stranded renewable energy. Roberts invokes Jevons' paradox: as AI compute becomes faster and more efficient, demand for data and tokens will grow exponentially rather than plateau.
