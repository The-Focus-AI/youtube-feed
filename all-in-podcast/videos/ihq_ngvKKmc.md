# Nate Silver Predicts: Democrats Take the House, Newsom Is Fading, President AOC?!

## Summary

Statistician and Silver Bulletin author Nate Silver joins the Besties for a wide-ranging interview that opens on the Knicks' championship run — Silver's bets, why Mike Brown and Karl-Anthony Towns were the "deltas" that changed the team — before turning to the machinery of American elections. On California's controversial late-surging mail-in ballots, Silver is blunt: there is no evidence of fraud, and the "blue shifts" people find suspicious are the predictable consequence of Democrats systematically voting later and by mail. But he pairs that with a harsh verdict on the state's process, calling it "completely unacceptable" and a sign of institutional atrophy that the technological leader of the world counts votes slower than India, France, or the UK.

Silver then maps the internal civil war of the Democratic Party into three factions: the activist Left (Zohran Mamdani, AOC, Bernie Sanders), the market-oriented "abundance libs" (named for Ezra Klein) who obsess over governance efficiency, and the "resistance libs" who prioritize partisan fighting and establishment loyalty — Gavin Newsom's base. He argues partisanship is the "gravity" of US politics, so entrenched that the outcome of 43 of 50 states can be predicted years out with 97% confidence. He flags a warning sign for Democrats: entrepreneurial immigrant groups, especially Hispanic and Asian American voters, are drifting away, while the "socialist" brand grows more popular with voters under 40 who carry heavy debt and lack an economic foothold.

On the elections themselves, Silver puts Democrats at 85-90% to take the House in 2026 — driven by an unpopular president at 38% approval, economic anxiety, and the long history of midterm backlash — while calling the Senate a genuine toss-up that could hinge on gas prices and the Iran conflict. Looking to 2028, he sees an electorate exhausted by geriatric leadership and hungry for generational change. Newsom, he reports, is actually fading in the polls (25% to 15% in primary surveys) as he tethers himself to Biden's legacy, opening the door to fresher faces like Jon Ossoff — or, as David Sacks bets, Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez.

## Key Points

- **Prediction markets over sportsbooks** ([3:55](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=235s)) - Silver, a Polymarket consultant, says DraftKings and MGM limit sharp bettors while prediction markets don't, so he's gravitated there.
- **KAT and Mike Brown were the "deltas"** ([5:19](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=319s)) - Silver credits the Knicks' turnaround to a defensive system that improved to the best in the NBA in the playoffs, plus KAT's offensive revelation.
- **No evidence of fraud in California** ([10:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=646s)) - The mistake is assuming each ballot arrives independently; Democrats systematically vote later and by mail, producing predictable "blue shifts."
- **California is a "failed state" on counting speed** ([21:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=1260s)) - That the technological leader of the world can't count votes fast is "a sign of atrophy of the efficacy of the system."
- **The US counts slower than everyone** ([19:23](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=1163s)) - France, the UK, Japan, and India know results within hours; recount thresholds almost never overturn margins above 0.05%.
- **Voter ID is reasonable** ([15:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=941s)) - Silver isn't against voter ID, but says it doesn't explain California; higher turnout no longer clearly benefits Democrats, who now overperform in low-turnout specials.
- **Partisanship is "gravity"** ([21:45](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=1305s)) - 43 of 50 states are predictable years out with 97% confidence; polarization, not rigging, drives it.
- **The three Democratic factions** ([26:13](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=1573s)) - The activist Left (Zohran, AOC, Bernie), the "abundance libs" (Ezra Klein), and the "resistance libs" who fight for the establishment.
- **Newsom is the "resistance lib" avatar** ([27:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=1620s)) - His embrace of the Bidens signals to a base that believes Biden was railroaded and the media, not the candidate, lost 2024.
- **Change elections and incumbent fatigue** ([30:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=1800s)) - Almost every election is now a change election; Silver compares the US to the UK cycling through prime ministers.
- **Trump approval at 38%** ([30:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=1800s)) - Anger over Iran, gas prices, and fatigue with a "singular center star" make it an uphill battle for the GOP.
- **The socialism brand skews young** ([33:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=1980s)) - Older voters associate socialism with communism's failures; younger cohorts view the brand far more favorably.
- **Immigrant groups drifting from Democrats** ([33:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=1980s)) - The biggest shifts are among Hispanic and Asian American voters — the most entrepreneurial, small-business-owning groups.
- **The winning 2028 playbook** ([36:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=2190s)) - Anti-oligarch rhetoric plus moderation on culture-war issues, supporting small business, and appealing to young men who want control of their destiny.
- **The age-40 cut point on capitalism** ([42:09](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=2529s)) - Roughly those who were in college around 9/11 and after came of age without feeling the American system worked for them.
- **Social media squishes politics into one dimension** ([46:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=2789s)) - Twitter is an effective mechanism for forcing a correlated stance on taxes, abortion, and Gaza; any heterodoxy gets punished.
- **The death of the chronological feed** ([46:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=2789s)) - Silver objects that following an account no longer guarantees you see its posts without feeding the algorithm another signal.
- **Midterm prediction: House 85-90%** ([49:57](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=2997s)) - Prediction markets say ~80-85%; Silver thinks it's higher given the headwinds facing the president's party.
- **The Senate is a numbers game** ([51:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=3092s)) - Democrats must win red-state seats; a risky Maine bet on Graham Platner (only two points ahead of Collins) could squander the map.
- **Gas prices and Iran are the wildcard** ([52:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=3151s)) - A visible drop at the Chevron station could shift the House; the Iran quagmire's length is the big uncertainty.
- **House and Senate are near-perfectly correlated** ([54:09](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=3249s)) - The odds of Democrats winning the Senate but not the House are ~1%; a blue Senate implies the House and governorships fall too.
- **Newsom is collapsing** ([55:34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=3334s)) - Down from 25% to 15% in primary polls and 33% to 22% on Polymarket as Ossoff-type younger candidates rise.
- **AOC as the outsider** ([57:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ihq_ngvKKmc&t=3452s)) - Silver won't discount her; young voters increasingly ask why they'd care about a "lame" party brand at all.
