# Senators John Fetterman and Dave McCormick: Bipartisanship, Money in DC, Datacenters, Graham Platner

## Summary

Pennsylvania's two senators — Democrat John Fetterman and Republican Dave McCormick — join the Besties for a rare joint appearance built around a single premise: "country over party." Both were the only candidates in their respective cycles to flip a Senate seat, and both did it by assembling the same working-families coalition of electricians, pipe fitters, and steamfitters that also broke for Trump. They describe Pennsylvania as a microcosm of the country — two big blue urban centers, a wide red rural belt, nineteen electoral votes — where voters simply expect their politicians to find common ground on energy, fentanyl, drone technology, and antisemitism.

The conversation's throughline is the intersection of AI, energy, and blue-collar jobs. McCormick recaps his $92 billion Energy and Innovation Summit and argues data centers are economic engines — citing Homer City's coal-to-gas conversion, US Steel, Eli Lilly, and a welder shortage where late-teens tradespeople clear $100k. Fetterman repeatedly breaks with his own party, calling a data-center moratorium a "China-first" policy and refusing to treat AI as "a scourge or a cancer." Both frame opposition as a mix of genuine not-knowing and a well-funded misinformation campaign they compare directly to the 15-year fight over fracking.

The senators also reckon with the failures of governance: record-low institutional trust, two historic government shutdowns, an existential debt problem, and the K-shaped economy that has made the last decade "the greatest 10 years in the history of humanity for those who have assets" while a $52,000 median-income Pennsylvanian sees no path to get ahead. Fetterman recants his old support for killing the filibuster ("we were so wrong"), condemns Maine candidate Graham Platner, and insists he would rather be primaried than lie. They close on money in politics — a combined ~$500 million spent on their two races, mostly to destroy each other's reputations.
