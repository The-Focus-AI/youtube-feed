# World's First Trillionaire, Anthropic Fable Banned, The New Oligarchs, Iran Peace Deal

## Summary

The Besties open with David Friedberg's fired-up thesis about a "Great American Politburo" — a class of politicians (he names Warren, Sanders, Ro Khanna) who dangle free childcare, education, and paychecks as "virtue" while eroding individual liberty and manufacturing "learned helplessness." Chamath grounds the abstraction in his own childhood on welfare in Canada, describing how a just-enough safety net let his father cycle between drinking and not working, and how the threshold for helplessness is "far lower than one may think." Sacks ties it to Pritzker's crypto-tax law in Illinois as the leading edge of the government dissolving private property.

The SpaceX IPO — the biggest of all time at $135/share, briefly a $2T+ market cap — becomes a meditation on where wealth actually comes from. Friedberg argues Elon didn't gain a dollar in the bank; the public simply re-priced "the machines that make the stuff," and Sacks and Chamath extend it into a makers-vs-takers framework where the true divide isn't rich vs. poor but builders vs. commentators. SpaceX also exercised its option to acquire Cursor for a stock deal effectively valued near $15B, and the group predicts an eventual Tesla–SpaceX merger. Jason pushes his long-running drum: retail should be allowed to buy private-company stock far earlier.

The centerpiece is the U.S. government's shutdown of Anthropic's Fable 5 (the guardrailed release of "Mythos"). Sacks gives a firsthand readout: Dario primed Washington to see Mythos as a cyber weapon, Anthropic over-extended the preview to a partner the White House viewed as a China-linked national-security risk, and when Amazon (as a testing partner) flagged a serious jailbreak, Dario reportedly refused to take Fable down until the letter arrived. Chamath warns the "own goal" hands hyperscalers a KYC-gatekeeper oligopoly; Friedberg reads a Claude-generated psychoanalysis of Dario ("epistemic exceptionalism… you've built a machine that outputs me"). The episode closes on the Iran MOU — enriched uranium removed, Strait of Hormuz reopened, sanctions lifted, no U.S. dollars owed — which Sacks defends as the pragmatic alternative to another forever war.
