# Bryan Johnson: I Just Took the Most Powerful Dose of DMT in the World... Here's What It Was Like

## Summary

Bryan Johnson joins David Friedberg and Jason Calacanis 48 hours after taking 5-MeO-DMT — what he calls the most powerful psychedelic on the planet, "somewhere between five and ten times more powerful than DMT" — to discuss the experience as a longevity intervention. Johnson describes being blasted into "infinite depth, infinite width, and dimensions" of raw consciousness, the dissolution of his ego and default mode network, and waking up the next day "laughing in a dream" — a characteristic of childhood. He frames psychedelics not as ritual or therapy for depression, but as a brand-new category of longevity medicine: a "psychological rejuvenation" that complements diet, sleep, and exercise by restoring the brain to a childlike, neuroplastic state.

The conversation drills into the underlying neuroscience: Johnson explains the brain like a globe of airports where psilocybin and 5-MeO scramble the routes, weakening the "New York to London" ruts of adult rumination and forcing new connections. He shares Kernel brain-interface data showing his default mode network "annihilated," along with a first-in-human metabolic reset where psilocybin pushed his blood glucose from the 99.5th into the 99.9th percentile. Friedberg presses on the philosophical fallout — if a drug rewires your neurons in hours, are you still you? — while Chamath argues that in an AI-accelerated world, that very plasticity flips from liability to asset.

The episode closes with Johnson's roadmap of next-generation interventions: mitochondrial transplantation (potentially using a sister's child as a maternal-line donor), Foxo3 plasmid gene therapy, Yamanaka-factor reprogramming via New Limit, and "Bryan Johnson organoids" — lab-grown copies of his own heart, liver, and lungs being used to test molecules in a dish before they go in his body. Johnson and the besties argue that as GLP-1s, longevity drugs, and psychedelics make biological abundance routine, the metabolic malaise driving political extremism and personal unhappiness in America may finally start to lift.

## Key Points

- **5-MeO-DMT as longevity protocol** ([0:59](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Axpnkqd-Agk&t=59s)) — Psychedelics were never on the longevity radar until Johnson's team found preclinical mouse evidence on psilocybin

- **Visual vs. felt experience** ([3:36](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Axpnkqd-Agk&t=216s)) — Unlike DMT, 5-MeO is not visual; it lock you into raw existence with "the gates of hell" feeling unless you surrender
- **Default mode network as ego engine** ([6:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Axpnkqd-Agk&t=387s)) — DMN constructs self and ego; stiffens with age, narrows experience into ruts

- **Quantified with Kernel + MRI + EEG** ([13:57](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Axpnkqd-Agk&t=837s)) — Structural MRI, functional MRI, Kernel optical brain interface, and real-time EEG to measure 5-MeO effects
