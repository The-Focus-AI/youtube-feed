# GameStop CEO Ryan Cohen's $56B Plan to Take Over eBay

## Summary

David Friedberg sits down with GameStop CEO Ryan Cohen for a wide-ranging interview that traces his path from founding Chewy to running GameStop, and now to a $56 billion bid to acquire and run eBay. Cohen walks through the Chewy playbook — competing head-to-head with Amazon on 30-pound bags of dog food in a low-margin "game of pennies," where the difference between failure and success was operating "pennies in the red" versus "pennies in the black." He credits negotiating suppliers so hard they never wanted to speak to him again, staying in Google AdWords until 4 or 5 a.m., and hiring "will over skill" — a team of "fellow psychopaths" who would go all in.

The middle of the conversation covers the GameStop turnaround. Cohen admits his first instinct — importing the Chewy e-commerce playbook wholesale — was "really, really stupid," and that once he became CEO he shifted to maniacal cost-cutting and leaned into the company's real strength: physical retail and the collectibles category, which is now roughly 40% of revenue. He frames GameStop as a business everyone loves to bet against, an underdog he was happy to "run into a burning house" for.

The back half is a detailed case for buying eBay. Cohen argues eBay "could have been Amazon" but has stagnated — every important metric down since COVID, active users off by 30 million, operating expenses over half of revenue, and alienated sellers who are "the customer" in a marketplace model. His three-part plan: pull $2 billion of costs out, dominate live commerce (a ~$400B TAM where eBay Live "sucks"), and build a first-of-its-kind marketplace providing liquidity for in-game digital items. eBay's board has rejected the offer and won't engage, which Cohen attributes to entrenched, overpaid management with no "skin in the game" — and a media that, in his telling, simply wants GameStop to fail.

## Key Points

- **From jewelry to pet food** ([2:04](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=124s)) - Cohen was about to launch an online jewelry site when a trip to a neighborhood pet store made him realize pet food was a recurring, fragmented, un-disrupted category.
- **Replicating the pet store online at scale** ([3:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=180s)) - Copy Amazon's supply-chain best practices plus the neighborhood store's product knowledge; a negative-working-capital business that reached billions in revenue without consuming much capital.
- **The competition was always Amazon** ([4:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=261s)) - A "game of pennies" where the market underestimated not the TAM but Chewy's execution.
- **Sticky cohorts and recurring items** ([6:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=375s)) - Handwritten holiday cards, pet portraits, and 24/7 service drove word-of-mouth referrals among pet owners.
- **Staying on top of everything** ([7:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=449s)) - In AdWords until 4 or 5 a.m.; a supplier saying he never wants to talk to you again is a compliment, gifts in the mail mean you're overpaying.
- **Will over skill** ([9:45](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=585s)) - A relentless customer-service hire from an old-folks' home who applied repeatedly became "incredible."
- **No regret selling Chewy** ([11:12](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=672s)) - "Nobody has a crystal ball"; the sale is why this conversation is about GameStop and not dog food.
- **Passive to activist at GameStop** ([13:14](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=794s)) - Management wanted him as a "patsy" to fend off another activist; crossing 5% forced the 13D-vs-13G decision.
- **Everyone hates GameStop** ([15:59](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=959s)) - He liked "running into a burning house" — pessimism and fear are where the opportunities are.
- **Original thesis: the console cycle** ([17:52](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=1072s)) - A cyclical business that does well early in a new PlayStation/Xbox cycle when hardware is tight.
- **The wrong strategy** ([19:42](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=1182s)) - Importing Chewy's e-commerce playbook was a mistake; as CEO he shifted to cost-cutting and pre-owned.
- **Collectibles as the winning category** ([21:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=1260s)) - A "totally different animal" from Chewy; overbought GameStop inventory (TVs) got stuck and marked down.
- **The trade-in model extends to cards** ([22:43](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=1363s)) - Bring in a PSA-graded 8-or-above card, get cash on the spot; resold in-store or online.
- **The financials** ([26:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=1601s)) - Collectibles ~40% of revenue, $350M in Q1, SG&A cut from $228M to $202M, $1.7B cash, $333M free cash flow, buyback authorized.
- **Why eBay is complementary** ([28:01](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=1681s)) - Secondary markets, collectibles, liquidity, and authentication overlap with GameStop — but eBay is global and inside his "circle of competence."
- **Keep the marketplace model** ([37:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=2241s)) - He would not take on first-party inventory; going head-to-head with Amazon on inventory isn't attractive.
- **Every metric is down** ([40:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=2430s)) - GMV down, active users down 30M, operating expenses over half of revenue, sellers forced to use third-party tools.
- **The seller is the customer** ([42:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=2520s)) - Get engineers on the phone with top sellers and fix pain points instead of hiring outside consultants.
- **The three-part plan** ([44:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=2681s)) - Cut $2B of costs, grow live commerce, and build a digital in-game marketplace.
- **eBay rejected the offer** ([50:07](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=3007s)) - The rejection cited financing uncertainty, but the financing comes off eBay's own balance sheet; little board engagement.
- **$500M of his own money** ([54:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=3286s)) - Cohen says he's putting $500M into the transaction and has never pulled a penny out of GameStop.
- **A $100M+ parachute** ([58:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=3497s)) - Cohen says eBay's CEO would collect over $100 million to walk away.
- **He won't go away** ([60:11](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4j9RPGLENNI&t=3611s)) - "I'm gonna do whatever I need to do in order to succeed" — many escalation paths in the toolkit.
