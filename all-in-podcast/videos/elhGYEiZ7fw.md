# Exiled Iranian Prince Reza Pahlavi: Transition Plan and the Fight for Iran's Freedom

## Summary

In this special part two of All-In Podcast episode 263, Jason Calacanis hosts an historic interview with Prince Reza Pahlavi, the exiled son of the last Shah of Iran, who has been living outside Iran for 47 years. With U.S. and Israeli military operations underway against the Iranian regime (Operation Epic Fury), Pahlavi discusses his vision for Iran's future as a secular democracy, his plan to lead a transitional government, and the conditions required for him to return to his homeland. He emphasizes that the intervention was a humanitarian rescue mission and that Iran, once the "South Korea of the region," became its "North Korea" under the current regime.

Shervin Pishevar, an Iranian-American venture capitalist and close friend of Calacanis, joins the conversation to provide additional context on the transition plan. Pishevar details a proposed timeline: a 100-day stabilization period, followed by six months to assemble a constitutional convention, and then 14 months to finalize a constitution and hold free elections. He shares his own deeply personal story of fleeing Iran as a child, with his father escaping on an Air France flight after 10 months in hiding, and the family later arriving in America with just $35. Pishevar frames a free, democratic Iran as potentially "the greatest peace dividend and economic dividend to be gifted to the world."

The episode also features brief contributions from Chamath Palihapitiya, David Sacks, and David Friedberg, who highlight the remarkable contributions of the Iranian diaspora to Silicon Valley -- naming figures like Dara Khosrowshahi (Uber), Pierre Omidyar (eBay), Omid Kordestani (Google), and Ali Ghodsi (Databricks). Pahlavi closes by expressing hope that the podcast could one day broadcast from Tehran, and the hosts enthusiastically agree.
