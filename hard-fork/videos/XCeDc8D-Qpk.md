# Who Is John Ternus, Apple's New C.E.O.?

## Summary

This episode of Hard Fork opens with the news that Tim Cook is stepping down as Apple's CEO after a 15-year run, transitioning into the role of executive chairman while Apple's senior vice president of hardware engineering, John Ternus, takes over. Kevin Roose and Casey Newton survey Cook's tenure: a market cap that grew roughly tenfold from $350 billion to $4 trillion, the surprise success of the Apple Watch as a health platform, the under-appreciated Apple Silicon bet that broke the company's dependency on Intel, and a remarkably scandal-free reign at a time when peer tech companies suffered through one crisis after another.

The conversation then shifts to the lowlights -- Apple's deep manufacturing dependency on China that became a geopolitical liability under Trump-era tariffs, the canceled $10 billion Titan self-driving car project that never even produced a prototype, the Vision Pro flop, and the broader miss of failing to identify a successor platform to the iPhone. Casey makes the case that Cook's biggest blind spot was AI: Apple is now an "AI laggard" that has to license Gemini from Google because it can't ship a competitive Siri, while Jony Ive and OpenAI explore hardware that could chip away at the iPhone's accessory ecosystem. Cook's political maneuvering -- the golden glass statue gift to Trump, the Melania VIP screening, the muted public response to Grok-generated nonconsensual imagery -- gets framed as both a successful tariff-relief strategy and a stain on his legacy.

The episode also features Andrew Yang discussing the return of Universal Basic Income as an answer to AI-driven job automation, plus a HatGPT round covering a pasta sauce that wants to record your family, a Chinese robot beating humans in a half-marathon, an AI-managed San Francisco store, Meta capturing employee keystrokes for AI training, and the SpaceX/Cursor $60 billion deal.

## Key Points

- **Tim Cook stepping down, John Ternus taking over** ([0:52](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XCeDc8D-Qpk&t=52s)) - Apple announced the leadership transition Monday; Cook becomes executive chairman, Ternus (SVP hardware engineering) becomes the next CEO
- **10x market cap growth under Cook** ([1:42](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XCeDc8D-Qpk&t=102s)) - Apple grew from $350B to ~$4T market cap, revenue nearly quadrupled, stock up roughly 2,000% since 2011
- **Apple Watch as Cook's signature product** ([2:44](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XCeDc8D-Qpk&t=164s)) - Initially dismissed as a luxury gadget, Cook iterated it into a mainstream health device with steps, blood oxygen, heart rhythm, and fall detection
- **Apple Silicon was the underrated risky bet** ([4:03](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XCeDc8D-Qpk&t=243s)) - Bringing chip design in-house and weaning off Intel gave Apple control of its destiny; Casey notes Intel is now partly government-owned because of how badly that pivot hurt them
- **Services growth a financial win, cultural loss** ([5:24](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XCeDc8D-Qpk&t=324s)) - Apple TV, Pay, Music became ~$100B businesses, but Casey argues subscription creep eroded user love and antitrust dynamics distorted the streaming market
- **Scandal-free tenure (mostly)** ([6:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XCeDc8D-Qpk&t=389s)) - Cook avoided the kind of crises that hit Meta and Google, with the lone exception being the 2014 U2 Songs of Innocence iCloud incident
- **China supply-chain dependency** ([9:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XCeDc8D-Qpk&t=550s)) - Cook's just-in-time logistics network was the envy of the industry but became a vulnerability as US-China tensions and Trump tariffs forced uncomfortable contortions
- **Titan car project: $10B and no prototype** ([10:52](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XCeDc8D-Qpk&t=652s)) - Apple's self-driving car bet was canceled in 2024 without ever shipping a working prototype; Kevin argues it was fundamentally a software flop
- **Vision Pro flop and the next-platform miss** ([13:19](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XCeDc8D-Qpk&t=799s)) - Cook's biggest macro miss is failing to find the next iPhone; Vision Pro didn't become it
- **Innovator's dilemma at Apple** ([14:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XCeDc8D-Qpk&t=872s)) - Casey argues Apple has no incentive to disrupt itself when the iPhone remains the most important and profitable computing platform in the world
- **Apple as AI laggard** ([15:02](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XCeDc8D-Qpk&t=902s)) - Apple Intelligence repeatedly delayed; Siri brain transplant still not shipped; Apple licenses Gemini from Google because it can't ship a competitive model
- **OpenAI/Jony Ive hardware as the iPhone-accessory threat** ([16:38](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XCeDc8D-Qpk&t=998s)) - Casey speculates AI-native devices (a desk gadget, a sweater pin) could chip away at the Watch and AirPods rather than the iPhone itself
- **Recruiting AI talent has gotten hard** ([18:08](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XCeDc8D-Qpk&t=1088s)) - Cook's "wait for AI to commoditize" bet means top researchers won't go to Apple because it's not an AI company in any meaningful way
- **Tim Cook's Trump diplomacy** ([19:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XCeDc8D-Qpk&t=1140s)) - Golden glass statue, Melania VIP screening, muted response to Grok image abuse -- effective at securing tariff relief, ugly as legacy
