# Was Hank Green Right About the AI Water Problem? We Asked This Environmentalist. | EP 168

## Summary

This episode of Hard Fork tackles the viral claim that AI is draining the world's water supply. Kevin Roose and Casey Newton bring on blogger Andy Masley -- a former high school physics teacher who runs the Substack "The Weird Turn Pro" and has spent 2025 on a one-person crusade to debunk what he calls a "made-up narrative" about AI water use. Masley argues that the famous "ChatGPT uses a bottle of water per email" stat is a misreading of a Washington Post article, and that the best current data from Google puts the on-site water cost of a chatbot prompt at about 0.26 milliliters -- meaning you'd have to send 8,000 prompts in a day just to raise your daily water footprint by 1%. He's careful to add that the picture for *power* is much worse: AI's share of the U.S. electric grid could rise from 0.5% to 5% by 2030, and coal plants are being kept open to feed data centers.

In the middle segment, the hosts unpack Australia's brand-new social media ban for anyone under 16, which went into effect this week across Facebook, Instagram, Threads, TikTok, Twitch, Snapchat, YouTube, X, Reddit, and Kick. Casey Newton explains how a single state premier's wife reading Jonathan Haidt's *The Anxious Generation* kicked off the law, why Roblox and Discord were conspicuously left off, and how Australian teenagers are now responding by exchanging phone numbers -- "this ancient technology that I didn't know if we'd ever see this again in my lifetime." Kevin and Casey debate whether the ban will work, comparing it to U.S. school phone bans and to historical age limits on alcohol and tobacco. Casey frames it as the moment social platforms have finally "lost the argument" about child safety in a democratic country.

The episode closes with the first-ever "Hard Fork Wrapped," a tongue-in-cheek year-end recap covering 2025's biggest stories: the Trump administration's executive order trying to preempt state AI laws (the Senate previously voted 99-1 against a similar moratorium); China as "other country of the year," with DeepSeek smuggling Nvidia Blackwell chips through third-party countries while Trump simultaneously approves H200 sales to China for a 25% government cut; and a coda on the explosive Roblox CEO interview that produced more listener feedback than any other 2025 episode -- including parents who said it changed how they think about letting their kids on the platform.

## Key Points

- **The viral "bottle of water per email" claim is a misread Washington Post article** ([6:51](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jCZIKA13KYI&t=411s)) - Andy Masley says it assumed 10-20 prompts per email, factored in evaporation from offsite hydroelectric reservoirs, and used 2020-era model efficiency
- **Median prompt water cost: ~0.26 mL onsite, ~2 mL with electricity factored in** ([6:51](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jCZIKA13KYI&t=411s)) - Google's data, against an average American daily water footprint of ~1,600 liters
- **Not buying one extra pair of jeans saves about 2 million prompts of water** ([9:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jCZIKA13KYI&t=540s)) - Andy's preferred way to put per-prompt numbers in context
- **2023 U.S. data center water use ≈ 8 towns of 15,000 people** ([10:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jCZIKA13KYI&t=621s)) - Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory data; Andy says the system can absorb this
- **By 2030, AI water use ≈ 1% of America's irrigated corn** ([13:07](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jCZIKA13KYI&t=787s)) - About 250 square miles of corn-equivalent water
- **Power case is the real concern, not water** ([14:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jCZIKA13KYI&t=886s)) - AI electricity share could grow 10x to ~5% of U.S. grid by 2030; coal plants staying open
- **Andy uneasy about being cited by David Sacks and AI accelerationists** ([16:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jCZIKA13KYI&t=1001s)) - "Some weird allies are going to pop up... not great, to be clear"
- **Animal agriculture dwarfs AI water use** ([21:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jCZIKA13KYI&t=1260s)) - About a quarter of an American's dietary water footprint goes to feed for animals
- **Data centers can fund water-infrastructure upgrades for poor communities** ([21:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jCZIKA13KYI&t=1260s)) - In some places, the bottleneck is aging lead pipes, not raw water supply
- **Hank Green's video gets a thumbs-up, with one nitpick** ([24:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jCZIKA13KYI&t=1440s)) - Andy thought Hank hedged too much; says even with the widest error bars he can't find a real per-prompt concern
- **Australia bans 10 apps for under-16s** ([30:16](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jCZIKA13KYI&t=1816s)) - Facebook, Instagram, Threads, TikTok, Twitch, Snapchat, YouTube, X, Reddit, Kick
- **Roblox and Discord conspicuously left off the list** ([32:37](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jCZIKA13KYI&t=1957s)) - Other platforms cry "picking winners and losers"; Roblox rolled out age estimation to stay off
