# She's Fought Online Spying for 30 Years. Here's What's Next.

## Summary

At Hard Fork's live San Francisco show, Kevin Roose and Casey Newton sit down with Cindy Cohn, the civil liberties lawyer who spent 11 years as executive director of the Electronic Frontier Foundation and is the author of "Privacy's Defender: My 30-Year Fight Against Digital Surveillance." Cohn — wearing her "Let's Sue the Government" shirt — traces the arc from her first big fight in the '90s defending cryptologist Daniel Bernstein against government restrictions on encrypted source code to today's still-unfinished encryption wars. Her side won the first round ("we have Signal, and we have HTTPS"), but the fight never ends: EARN IT in the US, messes in the UK and Australia, and a Canadian bill that Signal says would force it out of the country. Her sad conclusion: encryption, like freedom and speech, is something "you just have to fight for it all the time."

The conversation's sharpest theme is how the EFF's adversary changed. In the '90s, Cohn says, "we didn't anticipate that spying on everybody would become the number one business model of the internet." As surveillance became the business model, the once-united front between digital rights groups and Silicon Valley broke: companies that used to stand up for their users — on copyright balance, on Section 230 — now roll over on exceptions like FOSTA-SESTA and are increasingly adversarial to their own users. Her rule for tech companies is simple: stand with your users and EFF stands with you; stand against them and EFF is first in line to sue. On AI, she notes that the two hard lines Anthropic drew that got it in trouble with the Defense Department — mass domestic spying and autonomous weapons — are exactly right: mass surveillance supercharged by AI shifts power away from citizens, and the political economy "will turn on whether we can stop mass surveillance that's AI supercharged." She points to Dobbs prosecutions built on Facebook messages, social media vetting of visa applicants (which EFF is suing over), and a strange-bedfellows FISA Section 702 coalition from Ron Wyden to Rand Paul as proof the surveillance circles "are getting closer and closer to all of us."

Cohn also delivers one of the best rebuttals to privacy nihilism you'll hear: intelligence people will tell you old data has a short shelf life, so it's never game over — "if it were game over, they'd stop spying on us." On Sam Altman's proposal for chatbot privilege, she's lukewarm: better to build anonymity into AI tools so companies never hold the data than to expect them to protect us, because "if their promises are up against their business model, I think you know which one is gonna win." And on EFF's decision to leave X — shrinking reach, abuse aimed at her staff and the people EFF defended — she reframes it as a free speech act: "freedom of speech has to mean the right to leave," including the right not to hang out in the Nazi bar.

## Key Points

- **Robot dogs as creepy mass surveillance** ([0:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=46s)) - Cohn's problem with robot dogs at the live show: "they're taking photos all the time, and they're going to last forever" — mass surveillance in the form of a creepy dog
- **The Bernstein case and round one of the crypto wars** ([1:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=70s)) - Kevin recaps Cohn's '90s defense of cryptologist Daniel Bernstein against government restrictions on encrypted source code
- **What winning round one bought us** ([1:42](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=102s)) - "We have Signal, and we have HTTPS, and when you lose your phone, you don't lose all the data on it because it's encrypted"
- **The encryption fight is global and ongoing** ([1:42](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=102s)) - EARN IT in the US, "the UK is a mess, Australia is a mess," and Canada is debating a law that Signal says would force it to withdraw from the country
- **Encryption used against Meta in court** ([1:42](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=102s)) - In the Meta social media case, offering encryption was argued as evidence of a product defect
- **EFF's rule for tech companies** ([3:34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=214s)) - "If you stand with your users, we will stand with you. And if you stand against your users, we're going to be the first in line"
- **How the tech alliance eroded** ([4:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=286s)) - EFF once worked with companies on balanced copyright ("rip, mix, and burn") and Section 230; companies then rolled over on FOSTA-SESTA and stopped standing up even for their own rights
- **Ideology or money behind the rightward shift?** ([6:28](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=388s)) - Cohn can't tell: "I never count out money... as the driver for people who have devoted their lives to making money," and the billionaires are now in their own echo chamber
- **The real split is the top of tech vs. the rest of tech** ([7:55](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=475s)) - Many EFF members work inside these companies; the divide isn't tech vs. non-tech but billionaire leadership disconnected from workers who "still want to be in the business of making cool stuff"
- **Will AI founders walk their talk?** ([9:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=540s)) - On Anthropic and pledges like giving 80% away: "if they walk their talk, we're there with them, and if they're not, we're the ones who are probably going to be on the other side of the v in the lawsuit"
- **Why AI supercharges surveillance** ([9:54](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=594s)) - People with less power need privacy as protection against people with more power; AI-scaled surveillance can impact "our ability to vote out the people who we don't think are leaders"
- **The Dobbs effect on surveillance targets** ([11:42](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=702s)) - After Roe was overturned, people involved in reproductive health suddenly became surveillance targets; a Nebraska mother went to jail based on Facebook messages
- **The circles are closing in** ([12:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=720s)) - Visa holders, the undocumented, protesters, even people filming police (two were killed in Minnesota): "these circles are getting closer and closer to all of us"
- **The strange-bedfellows FISA 702 coalition** ([12:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=720s)) - Ron Wyden and Jamie Raskin on the left plus Freedom Caucus members Andy Biggs, Rand Paul, and Mike Lee all want the FBI to need a warrant before searching mass spying databases
- **EFF is suing over social media visa vetting** ([14:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=881s)) - The government now requires visa applicants to submit social media to be judged on protected First Amendment speech; EFF has an active lawsuit
- **The answer to privacy nihilism** ([16:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=975s)) - Old data has a short shelf life; the best time for a comprehensive privacy law was 10 years ago, "the second best time is today"
- **Nihilism as entitlement** ([16:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=975s)) - Believing nothing will happen to you or anyone you love "is a bit of a denial or entitled position" when grandmas are being swept up in immigration raids
- **Chatbot privilege vs. anonymity by design** ([18:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=1112s)) - On Sam Altman's chatbot-privilege idea: conceptually fine, but Cohn prefers AI tools that never hold identifiable data "so that they don't have anything that they can reveal about you"
- **Facebook's shrinking privacy promises** ([19:12](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=1152s)) - Facebook launched as "the privacy protective social network," then its terms of service promises shrank over time as the business model won
- **Why EFF left X** ([20:14](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVIAq9IP-mc&t=1214s)) - Shrinking reach for digital rights voices plus waves of abuse directed at EFF staff and the LGBTQ people its posts defended made staying "not worth the candle anymore"
