# Satya Nadella on A.I. Jobs: Humans Will Do the 'Glue Work'

## Summary

At Hard Fork's live San Francisco show, Kevin Roose and Casey Newton interview Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella in a wide-ranging conversation about the AI economy. Nadella's central argument is that AI has to stop being framed as "one thing" — one model from one of three firms — and instead become a general-purpose platform where "the economy is at the frontier, not a firm or a model." He compares the transition to electricity, and argues that a company touting an amazing model while the economy grows at 2% "is not going to end well." Along the way he previews Microsoft's agent-first hardware bet (Project Solara), "unmetered intelligence" running trillion-parameter models locally on the PC, and the "hard reset" coming to Xbox, whose entertainment Microsoft admits it has been subsidizing rather than monetizing — noting that more Xbox-game monetization happens on YouTube than at Microsoft.

The interview also revisits the 2023 OpenAI crisis — Kevin gifts Satya a fabricated "Microsoft Advanced AI Research" sweatshirt for the division that never had to exist — and Nadella reaffirms the partnership while touting Microsoft's own from-scratch MAI models. On jobs, he pushes back on wholesale replacement: just as the 1980s produced billions of "typists" doing knowledge work, AI will produce new named, waged roles. He coins "cognitive coverage" (the software-developer analog of test coverage) as the work of understanding and verifying repos full of agent-written code, and insists humans will keep doing the "glue work" that automation can't. On the AI backlash and data centers, he points to 20 years in Quincy, Washington — higher tax base, lower local taxes, more employment — as proof communities can benefit, while conceding "you can't deny that the perception is terrible."

On the bigger questions, Nadella is measured. He reframes his Dwarkesh-Patel benchmark of 10% GDP growth as an economics problem: real growth comes only when "the marginal cost of the token" matches the marginal value it produces — not from everyone "token maxing" for its own fun. He admits to being an addicted token-maxer himself (his latest project: a coding agent wired to Microsoft 365's Work IQ as an MCP server that keeps a repo in sync with out-of-band discussions), but champions Copilot's "auto-mode": don't use frontier models for non-frontier problems. Citing Joel Mokyr's history of technology, markets, and democracy acting as checks on one another, he closes on how "AGI-pilled" he is: he buys that closed-loop domains like coding and AI research will keep advancing, but is skeptical that traces of messy human knowledge work are enough to "RL my way in." AI is "in the pantheon of all technologies a big step up" — but not, he says, "the last technology we ever will invent."

## Key Points

- **From "one AI" to an AI ecosystem** ([0:59](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=59s)) - Nadella's framing shift: the goal is an economy driven by AI as a general-purpose technology, not a single frontier model or firm.
- **Build conference and the platform question** ([0:59](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=59s)) - Can Microsoft build the platform and tools so every enterprise in every country can operate at the frontier.
- **Project Solara / agent-first hardware** ([2:47](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=167s)) - New device form factors not "beholden to the old form factors," designed for a world of long-running agents.
- **Unmetered intelligence on the PC** ([3:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=180s)) - Jensen's RTX SOC with a petaflop of local compute; a Windows PC running a trillion-parameter model locally for 24x7 agents.
- **The nurse's "badge" device** ([3:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=180s)) - Example of ambient agent-first hardware: scan, speech input, turn output into a prompt.
- **Xbox hard reset** ([4:37](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=277s)) - 25th year of Xbox; new leader Sarah/Asha taking a fresh 100-day look at a sustainable business model amid rising component costs.
- **Semiconductor scarcity is temporal** ([6:55](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=415s)) - Cloud/AI-driven memory and chip shortages are raising consumer-electronics prices, but the permanent question is the Xbox model itself.
- **The OpenAI 2023 weekend** ([7:54](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=474s)) - Kevin gifts a fabricated "Microsoft Advanced AI Research" sweatshirt; Satya's clearest memory is India losing to Australia in cricket.
- **"Intelligence is log of compute"** ([9:34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=574s)) - The original bet on OpenAI's non-profit-turned-for-profit, which many inside Microsoft thought was "nuts."
- **What Microsoft got in the renegotiation** ([10:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=646s)) - Cap-table stake, large customer, IP rights through GPT-4, plus the flexibility to build its own MAI models.
- **Frontier goal is everyone's frontier** ([12:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=720s)) - A base model with reasoning and an agent loop that customers bring into their own RL; they keep the harness, context, and weights and can swap the model.
- **Human capital + token capital** ([12:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=720s)) - Nadella wants every balance sheet and income statement to carry both.
- **AI backlash and "everyone is a stakeholder"** ([13:50](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=830s)) - Can't offer unbelievable tech while taking people's jobs, water, and energy; the perception "is terrible."
- **20 years in Quincy, Washington** ([13:50](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=830s)) - Data-center town where the tax base rose, local taxes fell, and employment grew.
- **Metawork and metacognition** ([15:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=900s)) - New waged work will emerge; the GitHub app evolving into an "ADE" to manage 100 CLIs is an example.
- **3.5 billion typists** ([17:02](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=1022s)) - The 1980s wouldn't have believed everyone would type all day; each new role gets a name and wage support.
- **Cognitive coverage** ([18:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=1080s)) - The test-coverage analog for understanding and verifying repos of agent-written code.
- **The untrainable part / glue work** ([18:44](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=1124s)) - Citing Sarah Guo, human agency and ambition are the "untrainable" glue automation can't replace.
- **Government stakes in AI companies** ([20:20](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=1220s)) - Open to a US sovereign fund idea (as with Alaska's oil wealth), not opposed to the innovation.
- **Reframing the 10% GDP AGI benchmark** ([21:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=1287s)) - 10% growth arrives only when the marginal cost of a token matches its marginal value.
- **Auto-mode and Copilot economics** ([22:49](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=1369s)) - "Don't use frontier models for non-frontier problems"; match compute to the problem.
- **Satya's own token project** ([24:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=1440s)) - Work IQ (Microsoft 365's underlying database) wired as an MCP server so a coding agent keeps a repo in sync with out-of-band discussions.
- **Mokyr's parallel paths** ([25:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=1500s)) - Technology, markets, and democracy as mutual checks; the model must be redefined for the AI age.
- **How AGI-pilled?** ([27:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zqEZyHkXgh0&t=1620s)) - Buys closed-loop domains (coding, AI research); skeptical that traces of messy knowledge work are enough to close the loop.
