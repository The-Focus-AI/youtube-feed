# What Really Happened Behind Closed Doors at OpenAI

## Summary

This episode of Hard Fork features Ronan Farrow and Andrew Marantz of The New Yorker discussing their 16,000-word profile of Sam Altman titled "Can Sam Altman Be Trusted?" The piece is the result of months of reporting in which the authors talked to many people in and around Sam's orbit -- friends, enemies, and people who have been both -- to assemble what they describe as a "forensic" account of his career. The reporting produces what Kevin Roose calls "a really damning portrait" but one that also acknowledges Altman is the subject of legitimate smear campaigns by competitors, including Elon Musk's intermediaries circulating "spicy and unsubstantiated material" in Silicon Valley.

The conversation surfaces several new findings: that Altman was not, as he has long claimed, leaving Y Combinator of his own volition; that his relationships with Emirati and Saudi royals are deeper than previously reported; and -- most strikingly -- that the WilmerHale law firm investigation conducted as a condition of his return to OpenAI never actually produced a written report. Farrow and Marantz quote a Microsoft executive saying there is "a small but real chance" Altman is eventually remembered as "a Bernie Madoff or Sam Bankman-Fried level scammer," and an unnamed board member calling him "unconstrained by truth" with an "almost sociopathic lack of concern for the consequences" of deceiving people. The hosts and guests connect this pattern to a fresh report from The Information about CFO Sarah Friar reportedly being excluded from key meetings, suggesting the same dynamic is repeating in real time.

After the interview, Kevin and Casey switch gears for One Good Thing: Kevin gushes about NASA's Artemis II moon mission and the four astronauts -- Victor, Christina, Jeremy, and Reid -- whom he calls his Mount Rushmore, while Casey praises Acme Weather, a new app from the team behind the beloved Dark Sky that uses Waze-style community reporting to send notifications about local rainbows, beautiful sunsets, and the Aurora Borealis.
