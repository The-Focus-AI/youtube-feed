# How OpenAI Is Rewriting Its Future

## Summary

Kevin Roose and Casey Newton break down OpenAI's strategic reset week: a rewritten Microsoft partnership that frees OpenAI to work with other cloud providers, a new $50 billion expansion with Amazon's Bedrock platform, a scaled-back Stargate buildout, and a pivot toward an $8/month ChatGPT Go subscription. They also dig into the federal trial in Oakland where Elon Musk is suing OpenAI for "looting a charity," and what the lawsuit reveals about petty grudges driving the AI industry.

Dr. Adam Rodman returns to discuss the remarkably rapid adoption of AI in medicine. He explains how AI scribes and Open Evidence have gone from novel to commodity in under two years, walks through his "green light / yellow light / red light" framework for patients using ChatGPT, and weighs in on Mayo Clinic's RedMod system that can detect pancreatic cancer up to three years before diagnosis. The biggest concern, he says, isn't safety — it's de-skilling of doctors-in-training.

Finally, David Duvenaud joins to discuss Talkie, an experimental "vintage LLM" trained exclusively on data from before 1931. The project explores whether machines can learn to forecast the future by first being asked to predict from a known past, and reveals how period-specific language models surface both beautiful early-20th-century prose and the uglier prejudices of that era.

## Key Points

- **OpenAI's strategic reset week** ([0:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=30s)) - Microsoft deal rewrite, Amazon expansion, Stargate changes, ad-supported subscriptions, and the Musk trial all hit in one week.
- **Microsoft no longer holds OpenAI to its infrastructure** ([1:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=91s)) - Until this week, OpenAI could only serve models on Microsoft's maxed-out infrastructure; new deal lets them use any cloud.
- **The AGI clause is removed** ([1:55](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=115s)) - OpenAI will share revenue with Microsoft through 2030 regardless of benchmarks; AGI now "evaluated by vibes."
- **Amazon invests $50 billion in OpenAI** ([4:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=286s)) - OpenAI models and Codex coming to AWS Bedrock; OpenAI displacing Anthropic as Amazon's preferred model partner.
- **The new shape of the AI bubble debate** ([6:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=375s)) - Skepticism has flipped from "no demand for AI" to "they can't build infrastructure fast enough."
- **Stargate scales back** ([7:34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=454s)) - OpenAI halted data centers in UK and Norway, paused Abilene expansion; shifting to leasing third-party capacity ahead of a potential IPO.
- **The Go subscription pivot** ([11:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=701s)) - OpenAI projects $8/month ChatGPT Go subs to grow 36x to 112M while $20 Plus subs fall 80%.
- **The Musk trial begins in Oakland** ([15:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=900s)) - Only 2 of Musk's 26 claims survived to trial: unjust enrichment and breach of charitable trust.
- **OpenAI's "looting a charity" defense** ([16:44](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=1004s)) - For-profit business is still controlled by the non-profit foundation; 2017-2018 emails show Musk also wanted a for-profit then.
- **AI in medicine — fastest adoption of any med tech** ([25:36](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=1536s)) - Going from novel to commodity in under two years per Dr. Rodman.
- **AI scribes and Open Evidence** ([26:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=1570s)) - Over 40% of US doctors use Open Evidence; one million queries in a single 24-hour period reported in March.
- **AMA: 80%+ of physicians use AI professionally** ([30:58](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=1858s)) - But it's largely Bring-Your-Own-AI rather than hospital-mandated tools.
- **Rodman's green/yellow/red framework for patients** ([33:09](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=1989s)) - Green: diet/clinic prep. Yellow: exploring symptoms. Red: chemo or treatment decisions.
- **Cyberchondria warning for the health-maxers** ([35:51](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=2151s)) - Uploading hundreds of labs to ChatGPT mostly drives anxiety; no evidence of better outcomes yet.
- **Mayo Clinic's RedMod for pancreatic cancer** ([41:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=2460s)) - Detects subtle CT changes up to 3 years before a pancreatic cancer diagnosis.
- **AI's biggest gain will be access, not new drugs** ([42:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=2547s)) - Rodman: improving routine care for the underserved beats waiting for AI-discovered wonder drugs.
- **De-skilling is the real concern** ([44:38](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=2678s)) - Polish polyp-detection study showed skilled doctors lost 6 percentage points of unaided detection ability in 3 months.
- **Talkie: a vintage LLM trained only on pre-1931 data** ([49:23](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=2963s)) - Built by David Duvenaud, Nick Levine, and ex-OpenAI's Alec Radford (GPT-1 author).
- **Forecasting is the long-term motivation** ([52:25](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=3145s)) - Goal: build models with century-long forecasting track records to evaluate trustworthiness of machine predictions.
- **Data contamination remains a challenge** ([57:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=3420s)) - Talkie knows about FDR and Hitler because archival sources have wrong dates, prefaces, and historian footnotes mixed in.
- **Talkie produces "beautiful prose by a terrible person"** ([1:01:25](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3khLoSNCjWw&t=3685s)) - Period-accurate racism appears in some responses; team uses a modern model to flag warnings rather than filter training data.
