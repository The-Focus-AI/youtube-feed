# DHH: Future of Programming, AI, Ruby on Rails, Productivity & Parenting

## Summary

David Heinemeier Hansson, known as DHH, is a legend in the programming and tech world. Creator of Ruby on Rails, co-owner and CTO of 37signals (Basecamp, HEY), New York Times bestselling author of four books, and a race car driver who won his class at the 24 Hours of Le Mans. This epic 6+ hour conversation covers programming philosophy, the future of AI, why they left the cloud, Elon Musk, Apple's app store policies, and the art of productive work.

The conversation begins with DHH's strong opinions about cookie banners, JavaScript, and why most programmers are "CRUD monkeys." They discuss Ruby's beauty as a programming language, metaprogramming, dynamic vs static typing, and the Rails manifesto principles. DHH shares controversial views on why managers are useless, why meetings are toxic, and why small teams beat large ones. The episode includes personal topics like fatherhood and his passion for racing at the edge of adhesion.
