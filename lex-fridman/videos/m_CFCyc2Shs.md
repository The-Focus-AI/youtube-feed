# David Kirtley: Nuclear Fusion, Plasma Physics, and the Future of Energy

## Summary

David Kirtley is a nuclear fusion engineer and CEO of Helion Energy, a company working on building the world's first commercial fusion power plant by 2028. In this technical yet accessible conversation, David explains the fundamental difference between nuclear fission (splitting atoms, used in current nuclear power plants) and nuclear fusion (combining atoms, what powers the Sun). He discusses why fusion is so difficult to achieve - requiring temperatures of over 100 million degrees Celsius to make hydrogen atoms fuse - and why Helion's approach using pulsed fusion differs from traditional tokamak designs.

The conversation explores the safety of nuclear fusion (inherently safe because reactions stop if something goes wrong), the geopolitics of energy, extreme scenarios, and how fusion control and simulation works. David explains Helion's goal of producing the first commercial fusion electricity by 2028, discusses the massive energy needs of GPU clusters for AI, and explores civilization-scale topics like the Kardashev scale and the Fermi Paradox. If achieved, commercial fusion would fundamentally change what's possible for human civilization by providing virtually unlimited clean energy.
