# Jeff Kaplan: World of Warcraft, Overwatch, Blizzard, and Future of Gaming | Lex Fridman Podcast #493

## Summary

Jeff Kaplan, legendary game designer behind World of Warcraft and Overwatch, sits down with Lex Fridman for an epic five-hour conversation spanning his entire life journey -- from falling in love with Pac-Man and Zork as a kid in Southern California, to his failed writing career marked by 170 rejection letters, to his obsessive EverQuest days that ultimately led to a job at Blizzard Entertainment. Kaplan opens up about deeply personal struggles including alcoholism, depression, and electroconvulsive therapy, as well as the profound joy of finding community through gaming and building some of the most beloved video games of all time.

The conversation covers the inside story of how World of Warcraft revolutionized MMO design through quest-driven gameplay, the controversial Horde/Alliance faction split, and the emotional culture of early Blizzard. Kaplan reveals the full saga of Project Titan -- the ambitious follow-up to WoW that was ultimately cancelled -- and how its ashes gave birth to Overwatch in a frantic six-week pitch process. He discusses the creative philosophy behind Overwatch's hero design, the nightmarish complexity of matchmaking systems, and the corrosive effects of online toxicity on game developers.

In the final act, Kaplan shares the painful story of leaving Blizzard after 19 years as corporate pressures mounted, his period of mourning and recovery, and the creation of his new studio Kintsugiyama -- named after the Japanese art of repairing broken pottery with gold. He reveals his new game, The Legend of California, set in an alternate-history Gold Rush era on a mythical island version of California. The conversation closes with Kaplan naming Zelda: Breath of the Wild as the greatest game ever made, his view that AI is currently "an interesting fever dream" for game development, and a passionate plea for game developers to "stop giving it to these fucking corporate jackals."

## Key Points

- **Early love of gaming** ([4:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=271s)) - Jeff grew up in the golden era of coin-op arcade games in Southern California, starting with Pac-Man, Asteroids, and the Intellivision home console through his father's connection to Mattel
- **Zork and the power of imagination** ([7:42](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=462s)) - The text-based adventure game Zork remains one of Jeff's most beloved games, embodying the principle that imagination creates richer worlds than graphics
- **Failed writing career** ([19:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=1140s)) - Jeff pursued creative writing with deep passion, attending grad school in New York, but ultimately received over 170 rejection letters and burned all his manuscripts
- **Rejection letters at a financial cost** ([27:03](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=1623s)) - In the pre-internet era, submitting stories required physical postage including a self-addressed stamped envelope, meaning Jeff paid money for each rejection he received
- **EverQuest obsession** ([34:13](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=2053s)) - After giving up writing, Jeff poured eight-hour structured creative days entirely into EverQuest, becoming guild leader of Legacy of Steel, one of the top guilds on the server
- **Met his wife in EverQuest** ([43:11](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=2591s)) - Jeff met his wife through the game, and his entire career and family trace back to his EverQuest obsession, leading him to declare "I think I won the game"
- **Hired through EverQuest guild connections** ([51:55](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=3115s)) - His EverQuest guildmate "Ariel" turned out to be Rob Pardo, lead designer at Blizzard, who invited Jeff to interview after seeing his Half-Life level designs
- **Struggles with alcohol and depression** ([1:03:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=3780s)) - Jeff opens up about drinking, depression, therapy, and ultimately electroconvulsive therapy (ECT) as the intervention that helped break him out of his lowest point
- **Introverts need people more** ([1:06:16](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=3976s)) - Jeff reflects that introverts may need people even more than extroverts, they just don't always know how to engage, and gaming communities provided that crucial connection
- **Early Blizzard was fewer than 200 people** ([1:15:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=4500s)) - When Jeff joined in May 2002, Blizzard was a small studio run by three gamer-programmers (Mike Morhaime, Allen Adham, Frank Pearce) who protected developers from corporate interference
- **WoW's quest-driven design revolution** ([1:56:11](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=6971s)) - After an early playtest of Elwynn Forest, testers expected quests to continue forever, leading to the "oh shit moment" that fundamentally changed how MMOs deliver content through directed gameplay
- **Three types of fun** ([2:25:05](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=8705s)) - Jeff cites Sid Meier's framework: fun for the player, fun for the designer, and fun for the computer, warning designers against "ant farm" design where they optimize for their own entertainment
- **Online toxicity robs the world of great creations** ([3:00:52](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=10852s)) - Jeff makes a passionate call to action for people to support young creators, arguing that unchecked negativity drives talented developers out of the industry
- **Project Titan's massive scope** ([3:04:24](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=11064s)) - Titan was set in future Earth where players had day jobs and secret agent missions, with GTA-style driving, neighborhood building, and massive open world -- ultimately too ambitious to ship
- **Overwatch born from Titan's ashes** ([3:19:54](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=11994s)) - After Titan was cancelled, a small demoralized team had six weeks to pitch three game ideas, one of which became Overwatch, built on Titan's salvaged engine
- **McCree concept art was a Titan space prospector** ([3:25:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=12330s)) - The iconic Overwatch character McCree originated from concept art of a lone prospector in the StarCraft MMO pitch during the six-week brainstorm
- **Quake is the GOAT shooter** ([3:58:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=14297s)) - Jeff declares Quake the greatest first-person shooter of all time, though he has logged the most hours in Rust outside of his own games
- **Corporate pressure drove Jeff from Blizzard** ([4:15:50](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=15350s)) - Jeff felt increasingly losing control of Overwatch's direction as executive pressure mounted, particularly around Overwatch League costs and the shift away from PvE content
- **Kintsugiyama studio philosophy** ([4:52:54](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=17574s)) - Named after the Japanese art of repairing broken pottery with gold, reflecting Jeff's belief that beauty exists in imperfection and that scars should be celebrated rather than hidden
- **Zelda: Breath of the Wild is the greatest game ever** ([4:55:34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=17734s)) - Jeff names Breath of the Wild as the best game of all time, with Red Dead Redemption 2 also earning a spot on his personal shrine for its Tarantino-level dialogue
- **Small studios are the future of gaming** ([5:07:59](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9rF1CSSh-w&t=18479s)) - Jeff argues that the most compelling, innovative ideas will come from small studios, with big studios primarily acquiring them for new IP
