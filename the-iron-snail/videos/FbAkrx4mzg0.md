# Why Forest Rangers Still Wear This 75-Year-Old Boot.

## Summary

Michael Kristy travels to Intervale, New Hampshire to meet Adam, Chris, and the team behind Limmer Boots — a Bavarian-American boot maker often cited by industry icons (including Merrell founder Randy Merrell) as the gold standard hiking boot. Limmer's reputation rests on a unique single-piece-of-leather upper, a side seam relocated from the back of the heel for a more sculpted fit, and an "overbuilt" stack of three leather layers that totals five US quarters thick around the heel. The brand operates on two parallel tracks: a German-made "stock" program (run by Chris) and a custom New Hampshire program (run by Adam, who left a banking career and trained for years under Peter Limmer at less than minimum wage to take over the shop).

The narrative core of the video is Rob — an 80-year-old trail maintainer who first saw a pair of Limmers in the 1950s and waited nearly 70 years to own one. Michael surprises Rob with a custom pair, then watches as Rob suffers a stroke mid-process, recovers, complains the boots are punishingly stiff during break-in, and then bombs down a wet New Hampshire trail passing hikers four and five decades younger than him because he was "just having too much fun to stop."

The video's wider thesis is that Limmer's longevity comes from refusing to scale: every previous offer to buy the company was turned down by the family, until Peter Limmer found an apprentice (Adam) and a steward (Chris) who shared the obsession. Adam still pulls JD Salinger's, Ansel Adams's, and Yvon Chouinard's foot tracings out of archive boxes for grandchildren who walk into the shop, and the team quietly outfits "Gopher children" trail-building kids who can't afford boots of their own. The closing gesture: Rob agreed that when he hangs up his hat, his Limmers will be willed back to the shop and re-gifted to a young trail builder.

## Key Points

- **One-piece leather upper, six side stitches** ([0:51](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbAkrx4mzg0&t=51s)) - Limmer relocates the main seam from the back of the heel to the inside, allowing a sculpted heel fit, hidden seam from rock scuffs, and added rigidity under load.
- **Built for New Hampshire Fish and Game search-and-rescue** ([1:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbAkrx4mzg0&t=106s)) - Two flagship models: the "Standard" and the lighter "Lightweight," used by trail builders, SAR officers, and rangers.
- **Customer education baked in** ([1:23](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbAkrx4mzg0&t=83s)) - Limmer often has to teach customers how boots should fit and re-teach them how to lace and tie correctly.
- **Bavarian roots, Jamaica Plain start** ([6:51](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbAkrx4mzg0&t=411s)) - Founder Peter Limmer Sr. (b. 1895) trained as a master boot maker in Bavaria, emigrated to Jamaica Plain, MA, and patented the first US ski boot in 1939.
- **Limmer–Meindl partnership** ([8:44](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbAkrx4mzg0&t=524s)) - Peter Limmer's lifelong friendship with Lucas Meindl Sr. led to the modern Bavaria-made stock program now run by Chris.
- **Custom tracings archive going back a century** ([13:06](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbAkrx4mzg0&t=786s)) - Boxes contain foot tracings of every customer including J.D. Salinger, Ansel Adams, and Yvon Chouinard.
- **The "handshake" fit** ([17:09](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbAkrx4mzg0&t=1029s)) - Foam locks the heel in, the rest of the boot is meant to "hug" the foot snugly so laces don't have to run all the way to the toe.
- **Adam's career change** ([12:24](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbAkrx4mzg0&t=744s)) - Adam left banking, took below-minimum-wage pay for five years to apprentice under Peter Limmer and become custom-shop steward.
- **Chris's obsession** ([15:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbAkrx4mzg0&t=900s)) - Stock-side owner and former Philmont Scout Ranch ranger; flooded Michael with hundreds of archival photos when asked about Limmer history.
- **Multiple acquisition offers refused** ([12:11](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbAkrx4mzg0&t=731s)) - Every Limmer family member rejected sale offers from larger boot brands until they found the right successors.
- **Rob's stroke during the build** ([10:52](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbAkrx4mzg0&t=652s)) - Rob developed a flu, then a stroke, and a thrombectomy was started before the clot cleared on its own.
- **Break-in is brutal on purpose** ([19:38](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbAkrx4mzg0&t=1178s)) - Rob emails the chain that wearing the boots in the house is "making me grumpy" and wonders aloud if Limmer could even start a business today.
- **Rangers, scientists, judges, photographers** ([2:24](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbAkrx4mzg0&t=144s)) - Wearers include Yvon Chouinard, J.D. Salinger, Supreme Court justices, and (via Ansel Adams photographs) the Limmer mythos in early outdoor culture.
- **The Gopher children moment** ([23:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbAkrx4mzg0&t=1401s)) - Adam offers free boots to dirt-covered teenage trail builders working on the same trail Rob maintains.
- **The willed-back agreement** ([27:13](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbAkrx4mzg0&t=1633s)) - Rob agreed his Limmers will return to the shop after he retires, to be passed to a young trail builder.
