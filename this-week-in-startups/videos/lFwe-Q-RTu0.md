# From Blood Transfusions to Burritos, How Zipline is Automating Delivery | E2238

## Summary

This episode features two major interviews about the future of autonomous systems. First, Alex Wilhelm talks with Keller Cliffton, co-founder of Zipline, which just announced a $600 million raise at a $7.6 billion valuation. Zipline has evolved from delivering blood transfusions in Rwanda to delivering burritos in suburban America, operating more flights than United Airlines. Cliffton breaks down the origins, economics, and future of Zipline's drone delivery, arguing that delivering a burrito in a lightweight drone is fundamentally cheaper than using a 6,000-pound self-driving car. The company builds 20,000 drones per year in their own factory and is growing 75% month-over-month.

The second segment features Anastasis Germanidis, CTO and co-founder of Runway, who explains why the next frontier of generative AI is "world models" -- universal simulations that can generate realistic environments for training robots and autonomous systems. Runway, which started at NYU's Tisch School of Art, is looking to build simulations that unlock massive gains in robotics by generating the rare edge cases that could take years to find naturally. The episode concludes with Jason answering a founder Q&A about when to pivot or give up on a startup.
