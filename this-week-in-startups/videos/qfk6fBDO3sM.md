# SpaceX + xAI deal gets us one step closer to Musk Industries | E2243

## Summary

This episode opens with breaking news: SpaceX has acquired xAI, forming a mega-corporation that plans to launch data centers into space. Jason and Alex Wilhelm debate whether Tesla will eventually join, fulfilling Elon Musk's destiny to create his own "Musk Industries" -- a real-life version of Tony Stark's empire. The discussion explores the economics of generating compute in space versus on Earth and the strategic implications of merging a rocket company with an AI lab.

The second half continues the show's "OpenClaw Mania" series, with team members Oliver Korzen and Lukas Durand presenting their top 6 favorite OpenClaw skills. These include a Reddit reader skill for tracking trends, a TWiST episode summarizer that costs just 18 cents per episode, an automated Gamma deck creator, Polymarket data integration, and Matt Van Horn's self-improving "Last 30 Days" skill. The discussion highlights how AI agents are now playing poker together on ClawPoker and monitoring their own task execution. Jason offers advice to OpenClaw creator Peter Steinberger and debates potential monetization strategies for the open source project, while also exploring the security risks of giving AI agents too many system authorizations.
