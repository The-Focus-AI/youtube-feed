# China Kills Meta / Manus Deal (Story Of The Year) | E2281

## Summary

Jason Calacanis and Lon Harris unpack two seismic AI-industry stories. First, Microsoft and OpenAI restructure their partnership: Microsoft drops its exclusive rights to resell OpenAI models, freeing OpenAI to pursue deals with Amazon and others, while Microsoft locks in a roughly 27% equity stake and revenue share through 2030 regardless of AGI. Jason frames Microsoft's bet as "perhaps the greatest investment of all time," potentially worth hundreds of billions in incremental equity value if OpenAI scales to multi-trillion dollar territory. Lon characterizes the new arrangement as "polyamorous" Big Tech.

The bigger story, in Jason's view, is China's National Development and Reform Commission blocking Meta's attempted acquisition of Manus, a Benchmark-backed AI startup that had moved its HQ to Singapore. Jason calls this "the story of the year" and "the end of Singapore washing" — a clear signal that the CCP considers Chinese-founded IP and talent to be state property regardless of corporate domicile. The hosts trace the implications: founders trapped, Meta's AI talent strategy disrupted, and a new front in the US-China AI war that could eventually entangle Taiwan, TikTok, and Trump.

The episode also dissects two self-driving car incidents — a Huawei HarmonyOS vehicle hitting a child in China and a Waymo grazing a child in Santa Monica — arguing that society demands superhuman, not merely human-equivalent, reaction times from autonomous systems. The "Off Duty" segment covers Russell Brand's bizarre Piers Morgan appearance promoting his Christianity book, an "AI slop" trend of feeding palm prints into ChatGPT for digital palm readings (with attendant biometric privacy risks), and a review of the BBC crime drama Criminal Record.
