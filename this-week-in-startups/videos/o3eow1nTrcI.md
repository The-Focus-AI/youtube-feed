# Why SpaceX Buying Cursor Changes Everything

## Summary

This June 2026 venture-capital roundtable from This Week in Startups centers on SpaceX's $60 billion acquisition of the AI coding platform Cursor. Jason Calacanis and the panel frame the deal as a strategic move by Elon Musk to control the developer IDE and pair it with SpaceX's "Colossus" compute. The backstory is a cautionary tale: Cursor was built on Claude, Anthropic saw how much of its token volume came from Cursor, then released Claude Code and effectively "shivved" its own partner. That platform-risk dynamic — where a foundation-model provider studies partner usage and absorbs the winning application into a native feature — becomes the episode's through-line, complete with a blunt warning to Y Combinator founders not to take OpenAI's free-token-for-equity deals.

The technical thesis that follows is a bet on local compute and "headless" AI. Jason predicts that by 2027 developers will move away from centralized frontier models toward high-performance local workstations (the AMD Ryzen AI Halo, Exo Labs' daisy-chained Macs) running open-source models like DeepSeek and Kimi. He argues teams should build a model "switcher" and a "maestro" layer — pointing to Perplexity's Model Council and OpenRouter's Model Fusion — so jobs route to the cheapest capable model and cost trends toward free. Tokens, in his framing, become a commodity "like hard drives and bandwidth," and value accumulates at the application layer.

The back half turns to the state of venture: OpenAI's leaked financials, the J-curve economics of Uber and YouTube, the drop in seed-to-Series-A graduation from ~50% to ~25%, and the "SaaS-pocalypse" of high-revenue companies trapped without exits. Jason lays out his "four Ds" (deal flow, decisions, doubling down, distributions), argues that unvarnished "tough love" is the most founder-friendly thing an investor can do, and makes the case that the return of M&A is what makes venture work again. The show closes with a review of Snap's new $2,200 AR Specs — a technically impressive but aesthetically "chunky" waypoint toward an inevitable AR future — and a somber tribute to the late Austin tech champion Joshua Baer of Capital Factory.

## Key Points

- **SpaceX acquires Cursor for $60B** ([2:24](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=144s)) - Deal closed right after SpaceX's IPO; Cursor's revenue hit a $4B run rate, roughly a 15x multiple the panel calls cheap.
- **Anthropic "shivved" Cursor** ([3:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=180s)) - After Cursor built on Claude, Anthropic saw the token volume, launched Claude Code, and left Cursor with no compute and no foundation model.
- **Colossus gives Cursor unlimited compute** ([3:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=180s)) - Elon, behind in the LLM race, pairs SpaceX's compute with Cursor's IDE — "peanut butter chocolate."
- **The golden age of M&A** ([3:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=180s)) - With "the wrath of Lina Khan over," Jason argues venture is back because M&A is back on the menu; predicts Elon could next buy Uber.
- **Cursor's negative gross margin** ([6:38](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=398s)) - Reported ~-23% gross margin, echoing YouTube, which was deeply negative when Google bought it in 2008 for $1.6B.
- **Don't take OpenAI's free-token equity deals** ([11:12](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=672s)) - Jason warns YC founders that Sam Altman's token-for-equity offers let OpenAI study usage and absorb the top five performers.
- **Local workstations return** ([15:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=900s)) - AMD's Ryzen AI Halo desktop (128GB RAM) and Exo Labs' daisy-chained Macs point to running open-source LLMs locally, keeping data in-house.
- **Use open-source models out of the box** ([25:24](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=1524s)) - "Easy peasy lemon squeezy" — Jason advises just running Kimi or DeepSeek rather than the labor of fine-tuning.
- **The headless / switcher / maestro thesis** ([24:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=1440s)) - Keep your harness and data, swap frontier models via a router; Perplexity Model Council fires three LLMs and compares consensus vs. divergence.
- **OpenAI's leaked financials** ([33:44](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=2024s)) - Revenue growth is unsurprising; the notable story is the "massive improvement in gross margin," partly obscured by free Microsoft Azure credits.
- **The J-curve, Uber-style** ([35:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=2110s)) - Jason's classic CNBC anecdote: Uber lost ~$2/ride, but a $3 price bump wouldn't lose addicted customers — "the boiling of the frog."
- **Tokens as a commodity** ([36:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=2160s)) - Trillions will be invested, but tokens likely commoditize like hard drives and bandwidth; ~90% of jobs run free locally on open source.
- **Where does value accumulate?** ([39:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=2361s)) - Nvidia card, frontier model, or app layer? Jason bets the app layer; the panel notes hardware still gets a good run.
- **Uber's AI budget** ([41:47](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=2507s)) - "Uber burned through its entire 2026 AI budget in four months" — the tip of the spear for enterprise token spend.
- **B2B beats consumer** ([43:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=2611s)) - Consumer ChatGPT users spend little; B2B deals (ingesting PDFs, automating decisions) are far more profitable — see portfolio co Hanover Park.
- **Seed graduation rate halved** ([48:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=2880s)) - Seed-to-Series-A "pull-through" fell from ~50% to ~25% post-ZIRP; the panel debates whether that's winter or just normal.
- **TVPI easy, DPI hard** ([57:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=3420s)) - Paper markups are easy; actually returning capital is brutal — a sovereign fund told Jason it prefers 15% corporate paper to venture.
- **SaaS-pocalypse & Mag 7 → Mag 70** ([1:12:02](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=4322s)) - High-revenue SaaS trapped without exits; Jason blasts the Figma/Adobe block and argues sub-$250B companies should be free to merge.
- **Undiscovered gems** ([1:06:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=3960s)) - Great prices come from first-time founders with no signal or "great but damaged" founders (e.g., Parker Conrad pre-Rippling).
- **The Whisper Network** ([1:19:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=4761s)) - Bling's internal software tracks intros and breakouts; Jason texts Roelof and Antonio Gracias his top picks (Micro1, Abacus, Autolane).
- **Tough love is founder-friendly** ([1:22:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=4949s)) - Unvarnished feedback beats coddling; Ben's framing: outline options A/B/C, let the founder decide, then "get in the boat and row."
- **Snap's AR Specs** ([1:32:22](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=5542s)) - $2,200, self-contained, "one generation away"; Jason calls Evan Spiegel a product genius who should run Snap for profitability.
- **Tribute to Josh Baer** ([1:38:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3eow1nTrcI&t=5921s)) - The episode closes remembering Capital Factory founder Joshua Baer, who died in a flight the day before taping.
