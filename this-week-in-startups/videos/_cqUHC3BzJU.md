# The Global Expansion of Self-Driving Vehicles

## Summary

This episode brings together three experts in the autonomous vehicle space for a comprehensive discussion about the state and future of self-driving technology. Ben Seidl of Autolane, Ming Maa of Moove, and Nathan Parker of EdgeCase each bring different perspectives -- from infrastructure and fleet management to safety validation and simulation testing.

The conversation covers how reliability of autonomous vehicles has dramatically improved, the challenges of bringing self-driving to new environments and operational design domains, and why the US is leading with regulatory clarity. Notably, the panel discusses why Tesla is not participating in California's AV programs, how autonomous systems will (and should not) interact with each other on the road, and what federal regulation needs to look like to accelerate adoption. The episode also explores the economics of fleet management through Moove's platform and EdgeCase's approach to validating autonomous systems through simulation and edge-case testing.
