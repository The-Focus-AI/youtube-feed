# Savvy is HQ Trivia + Wordle (feat. Scott Rogowsky) | E2245

## Summary

This episode features two distinct startup stories about building version 2.0 after learning from failure. The first half focuses on Scott Rogowsky, the beloved host of HQ Trivia, who reflects on the app's explosive rise from 50 players to over a million in months, and its subsequent collapse due to inexperienced leadership, technical instability, and a failure to iterate. Rogowsky reveals he was paid just $100/show and offered only 0.25% equity despite being the face of the brand. Now he is back as co-founder and CMO of Savvy, a live interactive word-puzzle game inspired by Wordle where the host plays against the audience -- a mechanic never before seen in mobile gaming.

The second segment features Trevor Bennett, co-founder of Starfish Space, which builds autonomous spacecraft called "Otters" designed to dock with satellites to extend their life or safely de-orbit them. Starfish recently demonstrated its autonomous rendezvous capabilities with the Remora mission and secured a $52.5 million contract with the Space Development Agency. Bennett explains how Starfish takes a "software-first" approach, using cheap cameras paired with sophisticated autonomous navigation instead of expensive sensors. He describes an emerging "space-to-space" economy where companies service other space assets rather than ground-based customers, representing a fundamental shift in how the space industry operates.

Both interviews highlight the importance of experienced leadership, sustainable business models, and the transition from selling hype or hardware to providing consistent, high-value services.
