# Why the Future of Video Games is Moving Back to the Dinner Table

## Summary

Two hardware founders, two very different frontiers. In the first half, Jason Calacanis interviews **Brynn Putnam**, CEO and founder of **Board** — a "face-to-face gaming console" built around a 24-inch touchscreen that lies flat on a table and recognizes physical game pieces. The pieces contain no electronics: Board uses a proprietary blend of capacitive and non-capacitive plastic plus an in-house embedded-AI software stack to track unlimited touch points and objects in real time, breaking past the 10-touch limit of conventional screens. Putnam — who previously founded the smart-mirror company Mirror and sold it to Lululemon roughly a week before COVID — walks through how she raised on a "hacky," barely-functional prototype ("fundraising is storytelling"), why she manufactured Mirror in Mexico but this product with a tier-one CM in Thailand, and Board's business model: a $399 console with seven games, ~$35 games with custom piece sets, and a forthcoming creator-tools subscription.

The conversation keeps circling back to Board's real thesis — category creation and getting families off isolating personal screens and back around a shared table. Putnam frames Board as "Nintendo meets Roblox meets Lego meets a great collectibles business," describes her daughter designing a mermaid piece in the Creator Studio, and explains her deliberate choice to steer away from simple ports (Monopoly, checkers) toward native IP experiences co-developed with the big franchise holders. Board sold out its 10,000-unit launch run in weeks and is now available at board.fun, with a Dungeons & Dragons tabletop product and a larger "board" on the roadmap.

The second half is Alex Wilhelm's interview with **AJ Piplica**, co-founder and chairman of **Hermeus**, which is building autonomous **hypersonic** aircraft (Mach 5). Piplica lays out the North Star — **Darkhorse**, a reusable uncrewed jet faster than the SR-71 — and the iterative **Quarterhorse** program that de-risks "one miracle per year," from a jet-powered go-kart (Mark 0) to a 30-second first flight (Mark 1) to a first supersonic flight (Mark 2.1) just 364 days later. He explains the **Chimera** combined-cycle engine (a pre-cooler that lets an off-the-shelf gas turbine survive Mach 3, then a transition to a ramjet), commercial dreams of cargo and trans-Atlantic-in-90-minutes travel, the just-closed $350M Series C led by Khosla, and the SpaceX-style "greatest hack in the book": getting customers (the DIU / Air Force) to fund your R&D by paying you to fly.

## Key Points

- **How Board sees pieces without electronics** ([2:34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=154s)) — Pieces use a unique conductive pattern in a special blend of capacitive and non-capacitive plastic; a custom embedded-AI stack tracks them from the touch sensor's raw data. Standard screens only recognize 10 touch points.
- **Proprietary, in-house models** ([3:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=180s)) — Models are trained on Board's own custom dataset; open-source frameworks are leveraged but the platform is built "entirely from the ground up."
- **Two years to launch with "the right Avengers"** ([4:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=255s)) — Putnam's Mirror background plus CTO Orion's computer-vision / self-driving experience gave a head start on screen-recognizes-pieces.
- **Manufacturing: Mexico then Thailand** ([9:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=581s)) — Mirror was produced in Mexico (weight/size made Asia shipping uneconomical); Board uses a tier-one CM in Thailand experienced with display products (Peloton, Portal, Nook).
- **Fundraising is storytelling** ([11:22](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=682s)) — Do the bare minimum to show the customer experience; don't over-engineer early startups.
- **Pricing and model** ([12:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=747s)) — $399 console with seven games, ~$35 games with custom piece sets, plus a creator-tools subscription launching end of year.
- **Engagement metrics** ([13:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=826s)) — Hardware sold at low-to-no margin; 85% of members are active monthly, playing 30+ sessions per month; LTV built on games, accessories, premium pieces, and creator membership.
- **The genesis of Board** ([21:37](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=1297s)) — A blended family of wildly different ages (a 3-year-old to 22-year-olds) that couldn't find one game for everyone; games like the Overcooked-style kitchen game let each age play a role.
- **No Kickstarter; sold out 10,000 units** ([25:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=1531s)) — Launched D2C for last holiday, sold the launch run within weeks; available now at board.fun.
- **Community marketplace** ([30:09](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=1809s)) — First community game (a pinball title) launches next month; a review-gated marketplace with per-game rev-share or outright buyouts.
- **IP strategy: native, not ports** ([31:54](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=1914s)) — In talks with all the major franchise holders; a "Monopoly Go" approach reimagining beloved IP natively for Board rather than porting Monopoly or checkers.
- **Darkhorse / Quarterhorse** ([41:33](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=2493s)) — Darkhorse is the uncrewed hypersonic North Star; Quarterhorse is the iterative de-risking program building ~one new airplane per year.
- **Why uncrewed** ([42:58](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=2578s)) — No pilots to be shot down and captured (the SR-71 existed because Gary Powers' U-2 was downed); decision-makers stay "on the loop," not in the cockpit.
- **The Chimera engine** ([50:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=3000s)) — A pre-cooler chills Mach-3 intake air (which would otherwise hit ~800°F and melt aluminum) so an off-the-shelf gas turbine survives, then transitions to a ramjet that uses shockwaves instead of mechanical compression.
- **Iterative flight progress** ([56:08](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=3368s)) — Quarterhorse 2.1's first supersonic flight (Mach 1.21) came 364 days after Mark 1's first flight; weapons rack already integrated.
- **$350M Series C and the SpaceX model** ([1:02:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=3741s)) — Khosla-led round; plans to stay private via extreme capital efficiency, getting customers (DIU / Air Force) to fund R&D the way SpaceX did with NASA's COTS contract.
- **The structural opportunity** ([1:06:26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQ5_y_pLgBk&t=3986s)) — No new US fighter-jet manufacturer since the 1950s (General Dynamics); the F-22 and F-35 each took 20–25 years and $30–60B to develop.
