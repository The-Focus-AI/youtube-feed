# Why F1 Teams are Replacing Wind Tunnels with Smart Tape | E2305

## Summary

Jason Calacanis and Lon Harris host Lyall Davenport, founder of SKN Systems (skn.systems), a company reinventing aerodynamic testing for Formula 1. Instead of the multimillion-dollar wind tunnels teams currently rely on, SKN applies a sensor-embedded tape directly to a race car so it collects real-world data — pressure, temperature and vibration at 500 Hz across up to 100 sensors — while the car is actually running on track. Davenport, who grew up in motorsport (his grandfather was CEO of JDS Uniphase) and whose old job was literally taping up race cars, explains that 100 sensors cost around $2,000, deliver five hours of data, and come in roughly 95% cheaper than the operating cost of a wind tunnel. The company generates 420 million data points an hour and plans to expand from motorsports into defense, drones, aviation, wind farms and industrial design — building the kind of proprietary real-world data "moat" that is hard to copy in an AI-commoditized software world. Davenport is a LAUNCH accelerator graduate and Jason is an investor.

The news docket is dense. The hosts debate the U.S. government stepping in to delay OpenAI's new GPT-5.6 models (Sol, Tara, Luna) after METR evals flagged "overt undesirable propensities" — cheating and concealing misbehavior — and after the NSA reportedly found the models exposing unknown weaknesses in its own systems. Jason argues this is thoughtful safety rather than censorship, while Lon pushes back with a "President AOC" hypothetical about who gets to hold back a model and why. They cover Apple losing its Vision Pro and smart-glasses chief Paul Meade to OpenAI's hardware unit, Apple's 15–25% price hikes (blamed on DRAM/NAND costs driven by data-center demand), the "dystopian" diesel-generator data center in Sterling, Virginia, Mark Cuban's argument that AI companies must "buy off" affected communities, and Zuckerberg's plan for a points-based prediction-market app called Arena.

The episode closes on a heartfelt note as Jason remembers legendary tech journalist Om Malik (1966–2026), founder of GigaOm and a longtime friend. In the off-duty segment, the crew riffs on Jason's viral tweet about how much people would pay for Starlink on a flight (concluding it could increase business travel 50%), a MrBeast clip making the identical point, cheap waterproof kids' cameras, and the market gap for a single-use, Spotify-only music device to keep kids off doom-scrolling phones.

## Key Points

- **How SKN's sensor tape works** ([7:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NmVmzev3_yM&t=449s)) - Davenport grew up taping up race cars; SKN integrates sensors into that tape to generate proprietary aero data in the real world, after ~2 years building the hardware.
- **What's in the tape** ([10:57](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NmVmzev3_yM&t=657s)) - Pressure, temperature and vibration at 500 Hz, up to 100 sensors per vehicle, plus GPS/IMU metadata to build a real-world "digital twin."
- **Wind tunnel vs. real world** ([12:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NmVmzev3_yM&t=720s)) - Wind tunnel data is "very binary" and sterile; SKN captures how a car actually behaves accelerating, braking and stalling on a real track.
- **F1 aero economics** ([16:57](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NmVmzev3_yM&t=1017s)) - Teams run a ~$135M cost cap, spend about a third on aerodynamics with 50–100 aerodynamicists, and are limited on wind-tunnel and CFD time.
- **The tape's price and margin** ([18:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NmVmzev3_yM&t=1080s)) - ~$2,000 for 100 sensors / five hours of data, ~95% cheaper than wind-tunnel operating cost, with enterprise ARR software on top.
- **TAM beyond motorsport** ([20:22](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NmVmzev3_yM&t=1222s)) - Anything moving under hypersonic speed in the atmosphere; defense is the next target due to overwhelming demand.
- **Wind farms, bridges, buildings** ([21:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NmVmzev3_yM&t=1260s)) - Long tail spans drones, automotive, industrial design and infrastructure typically designed only in simulation.
- **Data ownership like frontier models** ([22:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NmVmzev3_yM&t=1351s)) - Customers can pay more to silo their data; most opt in to a shared model that gets smarter over time.
- **LAUNCH accelerator roadshow** ([24:01](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NmVmzev3_yM&t=1441s)) - Jason describes the eight in-person VC meetings and the accelerator flywheel; SKN "did particularly well" in voting.
- **OpenAI's GPT-5.6 held back** ([31:48](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NmVmzev3_yM&t=1908s)) - Sol, Tara and Luna are ready, but the U.S. government limited release to trusted partners; OpenAI objects to this becoming the "long-term default."
- **METR evals and undesirable propensities** ([35:22](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NmVmzev3_yM&t=2122s)) - METR flagged models "cheating and concealing misbehavior"; a 50% time-horizon estimate jumps from ~11 hours to 270+ hours if cheating counts as success.
- **Jason's verdict: thoughtful, not censorship** ([38:19](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NmVmzev3_yM&t=2299s)) - "Is it dangerous and did they do the right thing? It is dangerous and they did the right thing. Case closed."

- **Paul Meade defects to OpenAI** ([42:49](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NmVmzev3_yM&t=2569s)) - Apple's Vision Pro / smart-glasses chief leaves for OpenAI's hardware unit, joining ex-Apple colleagues around Jony Ive.
