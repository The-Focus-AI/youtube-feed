# The US Government Just Shut Off Anthropic's Best AI. (What Really Happened)

## Summary

This episode of This Week in Startups opens with GRU Space (Galactic Resource Utilization) founder Skyler Chan in-studio, holding a brick made from simulated lunar soil. Chan lays out a plan to build the first hotel on the Moon using an on-site factory that mines lunar regolith with a robotic arm and binds the fine particulate with an Earth-shipped geopolymer — sidestepping the energy-hungry sintering and melting approaches. The pitch: the next SpaceX won't build rockets, it'll build habitats, and whoever figures out how to use resources off-Earth gets to own the land, Hudson's Bay Company style. Chan frames a milestone path from making the first brick on the Moon to earning NASA's ~$20B moon-base construction contracts, funded along the way by space tourism and $1M fully-refundable hotel deposits.

The back half turns to a live controversy: the US Commerce Department has ordered Anthropic to suspend access to its frontier Claude models — "Fable 5" and "Mythos 5" — for all foreign nationals over cyber-weapon and jailbreak fears, and Anthropic responded by shutting everyone out entirely. Jason Calacanis and Lon Harris debate whether this is a good actor self-regulating or political retribution against the one AI CEO, Dario Amodei, who never "bent the knee." They dig into David Sacks's account, an alleged jailbreak that Amazon's Andy Jassy is said to have flagged, conflicting Politico and Axios reporting on a "universal jailbreak," and the international backlash from a UK Labour MP and Canadian PM Mark Carney. Jason's throughline: no one should have a single point of failure — you need a multi-model AI harness with dynamic routing.

The show closes with the results of TWiST's $5,000 AI podcast-companion bounty. My AI Sidecast by Ricky Rivas wins $3,000 for fast, accurate live fact-checking, with Couchverse (Lemon Slice AI) and Convalenz (Beau Bratton) taking $1,000 honorable mentions — the latter surprising the hosts by turning a live read into presentation slides. Jason then teases the next bounty, Annotated.com, a permanent fair-use commentary layer for web content, and explains how he might incubate it inside his venture firm.

## Key Points

- **The next SpaceX builds habitats, not rockets** ([11:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZoK-prfL0sw&t=660s)) - Skyler Chan argues rockets are solved (per the SpaceX IPO); the next civilizational-scale company enables using resources off-Earth to build structures.
- **How the lunar brick is made** ([12:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZoK-prfL0sw&t=750s)) - A payload factory excavates regolith with a robotic arm, filters for fine particulate, and binds it with an Earth-shipped geopolymer instead of energy-hungry sintering or melting.
- **NASA's ~$20B moon-base signal** ([15:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZoK-prfL0sw&t=900s)) - An announcement around GRU's YC demo day that NASA would spend $20B on a moon base is framed as a huge demand signal.
- **The TRL contracting path** ([15:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZoK-prfL0sw&t=910s)) - GRU wants to be NASA's go-to construction contractor by proving Technology Readiness Level (1-9); demonstrating the tech on the Moon raises the odds of winning contracts.
- **Business model: own the land** ([16:13](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZoK-prfL0sw&t=973s)) - With little hard regulation on the Moon, GRU's roadmap is to own lunar land and resources, comparing it to the Hudson's Bay Company holding Canadian land for 200 years.
- **End state: Kardashev Type 3** ([16:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZoK-prfL0sw&t=1006s)) - The company's ultimate vision is galactic-scale in-situ resource utilization (ISRU), elevating humanity on the Kardashev scale.
- **Robots over humans** ([18:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZoK-prfL0sw&t=1080s)) - Building end-to-end with robots avoids the cost of human life-support (ECLSS), power, and comms; the brick is the first thing humans lack "flight heritage" on.
- **A third pillar: space tourism** ([21:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZoK-prfL0sw&t=1295s)) - Beyond governments and billionaire-backed companies, Chan pitches tourism as a third funding pillar; the hotel "indexes" comms, power, and rover providers.
- **The igloo: inflatable habitat + brick shield** ([24:52](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZoK-prfL0sw&t=1492s)) - Bricks are stacked around an inflatable, expandable habitat to shield guests from radiation and micrometeorites.
- **$1M refundable deposits** ([29:20](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZoK-prfL0sw&t=1760s)) - Modeled on Virgin Galactic and Blue Origin, deposits are fully refundable and reserve a spot; buyers can also etch their name on the payload sent to the Moon.
- **Moon as a stepping stone to Mars** ([35:04](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZoK-prfL0sw&t=2104s)) - The umbilical cord to Earth must eventually be severed; using resources on the Moon and Mars is the only path to permanent off-world life.
