# 3 AI Agents That Actually Replaced Human Jobs | E2272

## Summary

Day 70 of the OpenClaude era ("AO70"), and Jason Calacanis and Lon Harris bring back three of the show's most active power users -- Ryan Carson (Claw Chief), Alex Finn (Henry Intelligent Machines / Creator Buddy), and Yazin Al-Ibrahim (OpenOats / Sidecast) -- to react to Anthropic's Friday-night announcement that Claude Pro/Max subscriptions no longer cover third-party agentic tools. Starting Saturday April 4th, OpenClaude users moved to pay-as-you-go billing, with Anthropic exec Boris Cherny explaining the company is "prioritizing customers using our products and API." Ryan reveals one full day of running Claw Chief on Opus 4.6 was projecting $3,000-$6,000 per month, the panel agrees a $2,000/month consumer tier is inevitable, and Alex Finn bets his social media accounts that ChatGPT-5.5 ("Spud") and Claude's next model ("Mythos") will land at that price this year.

The middle of the show is a live "demo or die" parade. Yazin's Sidecast is a multi-persona sidebar (fact checker, archivist, sniper, menace) that streams real-time citations, archival callbacks, and one-liners to a podcast host's screen during the live show -- effectively replacing the Howard Stern crew of Jackie Martling, Gary Dell'Abate, John Hein, and Fred Norris. Ryan's Claw Chief v2 is an opinionated, cron-driven chief-of-staff protocol with executive-assistant and BizDev skills (his Claude has already booked three real meetings with people he's never spoken to). Alex Finn's Henry Intelligent Machines is an autonomous swarm of agents that scrapes Reddit, X, YouTube, Product Hunt, and message boards for opportunities, drafts ventures with market sizing and proposed plans, then launches them with budgeted Facebook/Instagram ad spend -- all gated by a single human approval step. Henry has already texted Alex overnight that it fixed bugs, built him a CRM, sketched new video ideas, and sent a nano-banana self-portrait of itself as an owl.

Around the demos, the panel maps the new economics: the LLM industry will need to pay back roughly $500B of paid-in capital from profits, OpenAI's acquisition of TBPN ("Podcast Bros Network") is dissected as Sam Altman buying immaculate vibes for a trillion-dollar company with bad PR, and Brex CTO Pedro Franceschi previews "Crab Trap" -- an HTTP-proxy LLM that polices another LLM at the network layer. Jason riffs on the Chinese "colleagues.skill" survivalist trend (workers automating each other to get them laid off first), the "caveman Claude" prompt that cuts tokens 75%, Higgsfield's "illegal undisclosed advertising" influencer playbook, and a 2008 PayPerPost / Ted Murphy / Izea TWiST flashback in which Jason paid Tyler $100 to write "PayPerPost" on his forehead live on air.
