# The Defense Tech Startup YC Kicked Out of a Meeting is Now Arming America | E2280

## Summary

In this Friday TWIST episode, Jason Calacanis, Alex Wilhelm, and Lon Harris dig into the strategic shift sweeping big tech: KPMG, Meta, and Nike all announced ~10,000-person layoffs, and Calacanis frames it as a "machines over people" inflection point. He argues that capital allocators like Zuckerberg are choosing AI infrastructure over headcount, and that displaced knowledge workers should pursue "distraction" problems -- markets too small (under 10%, 5%, even 1% of revenue) for incumbents like Google, Apple, Amazon, or Tesla to chase. The hosts also tee up a multi-month focus on Small Language Models (SLMs) and Vertical SLMs, expanding on the Ollama and Bittensor coverage that has run through the show.

The first guest is Will Edwards, founder of Firehawk Aerospace, the defense-tech startup that Y Combinator famously kicked out of an in-person interview in 2020 for being "defense" -- and which is now selling 3D-printed solid rocket motor (SRM) propellant to the Army, Air Force, Hanwha, and Raytheon. Edwards explains how their co-kneader feedstock turns a $30 million mixer + 12-hour batch + 2-month cure into a stamped or printed grain produced in minutes, 5x-ing US base bleed motor capacity, removing humans from a deadly ammonium perchlorate process, and addressing the missile shortage exposed by the Iran conflict. He's just acquired a 640-acre missile integration facility in Mississippi and frames defense as a 24-month window to become relevant before a decade-long growth cycle locks in.

The second guest is Maruchi Kim, the PhD-graduate creator of Viewbuds -- earbuds with stitched front-and-back cameras that pair with an on-device AI model. Calacanis pitches him on a series of go-to-market wedges (translation in foreign pharmacies, security/safety alerts for joggers and skiers, vision-impaired reading via Speechify-style narration, kids' Apple-Watch-style safety wearable) and offers him an accelerator slot. The episode closes with riffs on Pilatus PC-24 jets that can land on dirt roads, Tesla FSD atrophy, and the Plaud NotePin S as a wearable conversation-capture tool.

## Key Points

- **The "machines over people" thesis** ([7:26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=446s)) - Meta, KPMG, and Nike all announced ~10k layoffs while reallocating capital to AI infrastructure
- **Meta's Chief People Officer quote** ([5:37](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=337s)) - Lon reads Janelle Gale's memo: layoffs "to allow us to offset the other investments we're making" -- explicitly funding AI chip deals like the new Amazon Graviton agreement
- **The 10%/5%/1% distraction rule** ([9:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=586s)) - Find a problem that's under 10% of a giant's revenue; under 1% is the sweet spot where they can't justify their best people
- **Amazon brick-and-mortar as a "distraction" failure** ([10:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=621s)) - Disastrous because consumers didn't go; example of how big companies fail at side projects
- **SLMs and VSLMs as the next theme** ([0:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=15s)) - After Ollama and Bittensor, Calacanis is pointing TWIST listeners at small/vertical language models running locally on Macs and Dell GB300 workstations
- **Firehawk's co-kneader feedstock** ([17:04](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=1024s)) - Replaces $30M mixers and 12-hour batches with extruded energetic pellets that are stamped or 3D-printed into solid rocket motor grains
- **Iran war exposed missile shortages** ([21:28](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=1288s)) - First seven weeks of the conflict consumed a major chunk of US missile inventory, motivating new domestic production
- **640-acre Mississippi facility** ([21:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=1306s)) - Currently produces 50,000 motors/year, scaling to 120,000/year, with primes Hanwha and Raytheon as partners and cap-table investors
- **1% transformed: how far the US military is into modernization** ([25:05](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=1505s)) - Edwards estimates only ~1% of the $1.5T proposed budget reaches startups; "Ghost Murmur" tracking-by-heartbeat is still a Lockheed prime tool, not a startup
- **Components-business thesis for defense** ([28:05](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=1685s)) - Mastering propellant unlocks artillery shells, rockets, missiles, and drone-replacement munitions; Ukraine fired ~14M artillery shells before drones took over by necessity
- **"Gas station of rocket motors"** ([31:03](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=1863s)) - Feedstock-and-printers model means anyone in Korea or Europe could stand up a workshop with Firehawk's materials
- **Pilatus PC-24 + Cessna autoland** ([34:20](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=2060s)) - Single-pilot jets, push-button autoland in actual heart-attack rescue cases, parachutes, and PlaneSense / Surf Air membership models for the Bay Area / Austin / LA triangle
- **Tesla FSD atrophy** ([36:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=2189s)) - Calacanis admits "I was partially paying attention" when his FSD hit cones; 99% disengage rate has trained drivers to drift
- **Viewbuds technical core** ([41:11](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=2471s)) - PhD project bridging wearables and deep-learning networks; a $1-2 camera module + firmware that streams to a host device + open-source models that only became usable last year
- **Platform vs. vertical Viewbuds business model** ([42:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=2552s)) - License software/reference HW to OEMs (Bose, Sony, Apple) rather than build a new audio brand; Calacanis predicts a $50M acqui-hire flip
- **Translation, safety, vision-impaired reading** ([45:43](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=2743s)) - Foundation use cases: scanning Japanese pharmacy aisles, "on your six" safety alerts for joggers and skiers, Speechify-style book reading for grandmas with cataracts
- **"Today's toys are tomorrow's innovations"** ([54:54](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=3294s)) - Calacanis's angel-investing credo: never underestimate tinkerers, because the orthodontic retainer monitor and Viewbuds are how breakout products begin
- **GLP-1s have collapsed in price** ([37:54](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=2274s)) - From ~$2,400/month four years ago to ~$150-200/month now; "Slim Cal / J-Ranch" branding update from "Fat Cal"
- **Bluetooth bandwidth limits AI hardware** ([54:09](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RT09WX9_hqY&t=3249s)) - Two cameras barely streamed over Bluetooth; Wi-Fi draws too much power; the constraint pushes intelligence onto the buds themselves
