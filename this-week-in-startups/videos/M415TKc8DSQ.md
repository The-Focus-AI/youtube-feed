# Jason turned $11 and one tweet into 1.1M views | E2311

## Summary

Jason Calacanis (broadcasting from a sweltering, barely air-conditioned Paris Airbnb) and co-host Lon Harris open with a "tactical-practical" segment featuring guest Tibo Louis-Lucas — the Product Hunt "Maker of the Year," serial founder behind Tweet Hunter and Taplio, and now builder of Revid.ai. Tibo's core lesson: ship in public relentlessly. He built and shipped one product a week, failing through ten "shitty" projects before the 11th, Tweet Hunter, hit $1M ARR in under a year and sold to Lempire. The conversation doubles as a case study for Jason's own viral moment: an $11 AI tool called PodMeme (built with Perplexity Computer plus Grok 4.5) that deep-links the best segments across top podcasts into a single stream — a screenshot of which racked up 1.1 million views on X. Tibo shares a modern influencer playbook: skip the $10K sponsorship gamble, seed products to 50-100 micro-influencers, pay a small base fee (~$200) plus a high success bonus (~$500 for 50K views), and let the algorithm — not follower count — decide who goes viral. (Note: the interview-transcriber mislabels Tibo's origin-story segment around [8:07] as "Lon Harris"; the content is clearly Tibo speaking.)

The news block is dominated by Apple's 40-page trade-secret lawsuit against OpenAI, centered on former Apple hardware exec Tang Tan. Jason repeatedly insists Apple has OpenAI "dead to rights" (while guessing Sam Altman himself didn't know), and calls the fumbling of the Apple, New York Times, and Elon relationships strategic disasters — arguing each could have been a lucrative partnership. He improvises a mock pitch of how OpenAI should have bought its way to peace with the New York Times (a billion in equity plus $100M/year). The crew also digs into Washington DC's new robotaxi bill (Uber opposing, Waymo supporting), framing it as a managed "civil war" inside Uber, Lyft and DoorDash as they transition from human to autonomous fleets.

The back half covers a "We Must Act Now" open letter from 200 economists and 15 Nobel laureates on AI job displacement, Jason's compounding-percentage predictions (50% of rides automated by ~2032, plus a likely one-year pause after a fatal on-video accident), and a detailed teardown of Flock Safety's PR problem after its CEO called the DeFlock mapping project a "terrorist organization." Jason lays out, from first principles, exactly how Flock should communicate its data-retention and audit policies, before a running comedic bit ("Barry or Big Brother") over whether Lon would accept neighborhood surveillance to save a lost dog. The show closes with a tribute to actor Sam Neill.

## Key Points

- **Ship one product a week until something sticks** ([8:07](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=487s)) - Tibo's origin story: built and shipped weekly, failing repeatedly before Tweet Hunter hit (segment mislabeled as Lon).
- **Building in public forces a validation mindset** ([11:26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=686s)) - Tibo argues it shifts founders from "in love with my idea" to getting the hard no and moving to a better idea.
- **PodMeme built with Perplexity Computer + Grok 4.5** ([12:16](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=736s)) - Perplexity Computer as the harness, Grok 4.5 wrote it; scans top 25-75 podcasts and deep-links their timestamped segments.
- **$11 tool, 1.1 million views** ([12:45](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=765s)) - Jason's screenshot post about PodMeme became his viral case study for building in public.
- **Killer next feature: export to one MP3** ([13:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=801s)) - A condensed audio digest with "next up, this" transitions between segments.
- **Gave 25% profit-share to an influencer** ([14:44](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=884s)) - Tibo says builders and influencers both crave partnerships that rarely happen.
- **Compound the same audience across products** ([15:37](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=937s)) - Pick one target customer and problem (creator economy) so every product speaks to the same people.
- **Follower count is dead; the algorithm decides** ([18:28](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=1108s)) - Even a million followers won't get you shared if Grok's "For You" doesn't pick the post up.
- **Micro-influencer playbook: $200 base + $500 bonus** ([19:38](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=1178s)) - Agents find accounts averaging ~1K views, pay a small base plus a bonus if a post breaks 50K views, then recycle the winners.
- **Tibo banned on LinkedIn twice** ([21:58](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=1318s)) - Over Taplio, his LinkedIn management SaaS acquired by lemlist in 2022; leads to a rant on LinkedIn's anti-automation stance.
- **LinkedIn should just give everyone an email inbox** ([24:42](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=1482s)) - Lon says Rahul (Superhuman) pitched this; a billion @linkedin.com addresses would have been "the greatest product ever."
- **Apple has OpenAI "dead to rights"** ([30:03](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=1803s)) - Jason: professional orgs don't file front-page lawsuits unless the evidence is overwhelming; centers on Tang Tan.
- **Jony Ive's OpenAI equity worth billions** ([33:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=1980s)) - $6.4B all-equity acquisition; Jason estimates Ive now holds more value in OpenAI than he ever did at Apple.
- **How OpenAI should have handled the NYT** ([40:14](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=2414s)) - Jason's mock pitch: a billion in equity plus $100M/year for 10 years to make them "partner zero."
- **DC's robotaxi bill: Uber vs Waymo** ([44:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=2672s)) - Bill would allow driverless commercial operation; Uber wants a "hybrid model," Waymo supports full deployment.
- **A managed civil war inside Uber, Lyft, DoorDash** ([51:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=3060s)) - Jason frames the fight as internally managing a driver-to-robot transition, not company-vs-company.
- **"We Must Act Now" economist letter** ([53:07](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=3187s)) - 200 economists, 15 Nobel laureates, plus OpenAI and Anthropic chief economists warn on AI displacing white-collar jobs.
- **Prediction: 50% of rides automated by ~2032** ([60:33](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=3633s)) - Jason models doubling each year and predicts a likely one-to-two-year pause after a fatal on-camera accident.
- **Flock's "terrorist" PR blunder** ([62:45](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M415TKc8DSQ&t=3765s)) - CEO Garrett Langley called DeFlock a terrorist org; Jason maps out the surge-pricing-style first-principles fix.
