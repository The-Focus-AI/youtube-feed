# How decriminalization led to an explosion in cannabis startups | E2241

## Summary

TWiST's first Cannabis Roundtable brings together three top cannabis founders and investors: Ford Smith of Ultranative, Andrew Duffy of Sparkplug, and Socrates Rosenfeld of Jane (iheartjane.com). The panel explores how the regulatory environment -- particularly the move to Schedule III -- is reshaping the cannabis industry, creating both opportunities and challenges for startups. A central theme is how regulation itself shaped consumption patterns, with Socrates drawing a compelling parallel to moonshine during Prohibition to explain why cannabis potency has skyrocketed in the unregulated market.

The conversation takes a sharp turn into demographics, revealing that alcohol consumption is dropping dramatically among Gen Z, with many pointing to cannabis as a substitute. There are now more daily cannabis smokers than daily drinkers in the United States. The panel debates whether this shift is positive, with serious concerns raised about increasing potency and cannabis psychosis. Andrew Duffy attributes high-potency products to CPG brands chasing power users, while Ford Smith blames "flyover" operators unconcerned with long-term industry health. The episode provides a comprehensive look at an industry in rapid transformation, balancing entrepreneurial optimism with genuine public health concerns.
