# The 10M+ Bet on a Beanie That Reads Your Brain - Sabi & the Future of BCI | E2282

## Summary

Jason Calacanis opens with a tactical-practical management lesson borrowed from LowerMyBills founder Matt Coffin: make every team member "the CEO of their domain." He argues that if community, sales, or editorial isn't someone's number-one priority, it will never excel — so he's appointed Lon, Ricky, Lucas, Bianca, Maddie, and Kabir as owners of their respective slices, and is now spinning up dedicated X group chats (the "Noty Gang") to activate the top 1% of TWiST's audience as a community flywheel.

The headlining segment is a skeptical interview with Sabi co-founders Rahul Chhabra and Atmadeep Banerjee, who are building a non-invasive brain-computer interface in the form of a beanie. Their pitch: 100,000 bio-potential sensors on the scalp plus a brain foundation model can decode thought-to-text without surgery — a "GPS for the brain" approach that Vinod Khosla has backed with eight figures. Jason repeatedly "calls cap" on the technology, demands a demo, and challenges them to make the system say "Calacanis" before he believes it, but acknowledges that if it works, it's Professor X-level telekinesis.

The final act reviews three live submissions to Jason's $5,000 bounty for an AI "podcast sidebar" with real-time fact-checking and persona-driven commentary. After demoing Armchair, Pod Commentators/SideCast, and BMD Pat, Jason narrows the contest from four personas to two — a fact-checker with citations and a cynic — and announces a second $5,000 bounty for Annotated.com, a Chrome extension for fair-use clipping and commentary on web media that he describes as "an enhanced multimedia Delicious."
