# Master AI Video Marketing w/ Magnific CEO Joaquín Cuenca Abela | AI Basics

## Summary

Jason Calacanis sits down with Joaquín Cuenca Abela, co-founder and CEO of Magnific (formerly Freepik), for an AI Basics episode focused on how modern marketing teams can use AI video tools to do things that simply weren't possible before. Joaquín demos a post-apocalyptic San Francisco ad for *This Week in Startups* that he built single-handedly in 24 hours — complete with storyboards, character generation, a rusted-out Golden Gate Bridge, a tattered TWiST logo, and a dystopian Jason Calacanis playing the worn-down guru that a young entrepreneur seeks out for advice on rebuilding civilization.

The conversation digs into the economics of AI video: roughly 10 cents per second of output (about a dollar per second when you account for retries), versus the $10K–$20K per minute traditional production costs. Joaquín explains that the real budget shifts away from the platform and onto creative talent. Magnific blends its own image models (upscaler, skin enhancer) with state-of-the-art third-party models from Google and others. Hollywood studios — including the production behind *House of David* — and small marketing teams are both adopting the tools, and Joaquín argues AI is creating *more* film jobs by greenlighting projects that previously couldn't fit a budget.

The episode closes on creativity, individuality, and the future of hypertargeted ads. Joaquín believes AI will match the creativity of *some* humans but never replicate the individuality that makes a creator unique — which will drive *more* demand for great storytellers. Jason floats a Minority Report-style future where 100 versions of an ad are generated per city, and Joaquín explains why static localization works today but full video personalization still needs something like "Claude Code for video" — a harness that checks AI output and steers it back on track.
