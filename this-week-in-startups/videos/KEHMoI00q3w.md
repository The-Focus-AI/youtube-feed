# How the OpenClaw foundation bullet-proofed its future (w/Dave Morin) | E2257

## Summary

Dave Morin joins the show to explain how the OpenClaw Foundation came into existence and what its goals are. While Peter Steinberger retains directional control of the open-source project, sustaining it over time requires a corporate entity, well-heeled sponsors, and dedicated maintainers. Morin details how he got "Claw-Pilled," the foundation's governance structure, the economics of open-source maintainers, and his physical OpenClaw setup at home.

The episode also puts the day's critical AI stories to Morin: the Anthropic-DoW controversy, whether OpenAI and Anthropic are catching up to OpenClaw in agentic capabilities, and Apple's slow movement in AI. Two demos round out the show -- Runtools' CTO Greg Kara shows a 3D interface for OpenClaw that lets users watch their agents work in real time, and PickleWatch founder George Yameen demonstrates an agentic workflow comparing OpenClaw with Claude's Chrome plugin for cost-effectiveness. Jason closes with advice for founders about stumbling onto new ideas and his take on the Department of Welfare's handling of the Anthropic situation.
